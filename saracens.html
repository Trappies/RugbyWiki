    • About The Saracens

    Saracens Rugby Club, based in London, England, is a powerhouse in English and European rugby. Established in 1876, the club has a rich history and has been a dominant force in the professional era. Saracens competes in the Gallagher Premiership, the top tier of English rugby, and has consistently been a title contender. The club has also enjoyed success on the European stage, winning the prestigious Heineken Cup (now known as the Champions Cup) three times. Known for their strong team culture and commitment to player development, Saracens has produced numerous international stars. Despite facing challenges such as salary cap controversies, Saracens remains a formidable presence in the rugby world, embodying a legacy of excellence that extends over a century.

    Origins and Early Years

    Saracens, founded in 1876 by the Old Boys of the Philological School in Marylebone, London, traces its name to the perceived invincibility of Saladin's desert warriors. After amalgamating with the Crusaders in 1878, the club moved across various grounds until settling at Bramley Road in 1939. The pre-league era saw Saracens produce internationals, achieving success in the 1970s, including winning the 1972 Middlesex Cup. The professional era, marked by financial backing in 1995, led to notable signings like Michael Lynagh and Francois Pienaar. Despite challenges, Saracens secured the Tetley Bitter Cup in 1998, marking their first major silverware.

    Courage Leagues and Restructuring

    Responding to the challenge of the Courage Leagues in the early 1980s, Saracens, captained by Floyd Steadman and coached by Tony Russ, achieved a 100% record and won the second division in 1989. Despite losing key players, including Jason Leonard and Dean Ryan, they surprised by finishing fourth in the first division the following year. The leagues were restructured in 1992–93, leading to relegation for Saracens, but they bounced back, finishing as champions the next year. The 1990s saw the club navigating a seesaw existence, with ups and downs, including avoiding relegation in the first season of professional rugby in 1995–96.

    The Professional Era and Key Signings (1996–2000)

    In 1995, Saracens gained financial backing, enabling signings like Michael Lynagh, Philippe Sella, Francois Pienaar, and Kyran Bracken. Despite moving to Southbury Road, Enfield, they finished seventh in the 1996–97 season. A landmark move to Vicarage Road in 1997 marked the "year of the Fez," symbolizing the team's identity. The late 1990s brought success, with a Tetley Bitter Cup win in 1998, but they narrowly missed Heineken Cup qualification. The late 1990s and early 2000s saw Saracens facing challenges and restructuring but maintaining a strong presence in English rugby.

    Challenges and Changes (2000–2006)

    The 2000–01 season began with challenges, including defeats to Cardiff and injuries to key players. Despite a fifth-place finish, Saracens missed Heineken Cup qualification. The 2001–02 season saw changes, with established players leaving, and despite a reasonable start, Saracens struggled. Inconsistent performances and relegation fears characterized the season. The coaching reins passed to Buck Shelford in 2002–03, bringing new signings, but Saracens faced struggles and ended the season in 10th place. The 2004–05 season, marked by English-based player recruitment, saw a strong start, but a mid-season dip led to a 10th-place finish.

    Progress and Challenges (2006–07)

    The 2006–07 season saw Alan Gaffney appointed as coach, bringing positive results and a Premiership playoff spot. Despite a defeat in the final, the season represented progress. In 2007–08, Saracens signed All Black Chris Jack and saw improved performances. The season included a good run in the European Challenge Cup, but the team finished 10th in the Premiership. The following seasons saw Saracens facing challenges, coaching changes, and fluctuating performances, highlighting the club's journey through the ups and downs of professional rugby.

    Brendan Venter Era and Notable Achievements (2009–10)

    Brendan Venter's arrival in 2009 sparked controversy, with significant squad changes and a focus on South African players. The 2009–10 season started with a 10-match unbeaten run, including wins over London Irish and Northampton. A memorable victory over South Africa at Wembley generated publicity. Despite a post-Christmas slump, Saracens secured a Guinness Premiership Semi-Final spot, defeating Northampton. The 2010 Guinness Premiership Final against Leicester Tigers ended in heartbreak, but the season marked notable achievements, including a return to competitiveness on the domestic and European stages.

    2010–11: Premiership Champions

    Saracens' 2010–11 season was a rollercoaster, starting with a loss to London Irish but rebounding with four consecutive wins. Despite crashing out of the Heineken Cup, their domestic form was stellar, securing a home semi-final and a 12–10 victory against Leicester Tigers in the final. The Men in Black, led by Man of the Match Schalk Brits, became English champions for the first time, overcoming a determined Leicester side.

    2014–15: Premiership Champions

    Saracens' 2014–15 season showcased high-scoring victories, securing a fourth-place finish in the regular season. In the final against Bath, they scored three unanswered tries in the first half, winning 28–16. This marked the first time a team finished fourth and became Premiership champions. The season also saw success in the Anglo-Welsh Cup but a loss in the European Rugby Champions Cup semi-finals.

    2019: Relegation and Scandal

    Allegations of salary cap breaches emerged in 2019, leading to a 35-point deduction and a £5.3 million fine. Chairman Nigel Wray stepped down, and Saracens were relegated to the RFU Championship for the 2020–21 season. The club's lack of cooperation in a mid-season audit played a role in the severity of the punishment.

    CEO Changes and Further Deductions: 2019–20 Season

    In the aftermath, Edward Griffiths returned as CEO, but the struggles continued. Saracens faced a further 70-point deduction, ensuring a bottom finish. Lord Dyson's report detailed overspending, including property investments, and rejected the claim that the Salary Cap was unenforceable.

    2020/21 Season: Redemption and Promotion

    Despite the challenges, Saracens had a triumphant 2020/21 season, winning the RFU Championship and securing promotion back to the Gallagher Premiership. The team's success was built on strong teamwork, leadership from experienced players like Owen Farrell, Maro Itoje, and Billy Vunipola, and key victories, including a dominant 73–0 win over Coventry and a crucial 117–15 win over Ealing Trailfinders in the play-off final.

    2021/22 Season: Runners-Up in Gallagher Premiership

    Saracens' return to the Gallagher Premiership in the 2021/22 season was marked by success, finishing as runners-up. The squad's depth, rotation strategy, and contributions from world-class players like Maro Itoje, Owen Farrell, and Alex Goode were pivotal. Despite a defeat in the Premiership final to Leicester Tigers, Saracens demonstrated their prowess and resilience, setting the stage for future success.
