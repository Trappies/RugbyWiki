    • About The Exeter Chiefs

    The Exeter Chiefs are a professional rugby union club based in Exeter, Devon, England. Established in 1871, the club has a long and distinguished history. However, it wasn't until the professional era that the Chiefs gained significant prominence. They currently compete in the English Premiership, the top tier of English rugby. The team plays its home matches at Sandy Park, a stadium that has witnessed the Chiefs' rise to prominence in recent years. Known for their strong team ethos and powerful forward play, the Exeter Chiefs have achieved notable success in the Premiership, winning multiple titles and consistently performing at the highest level of English rugby. The club's distinctive emblem, featuring a Native American chief, adds a unique touch to their identity, and their passionate fan base contributes to the vibrant rugby culture in the region.

    Early Years

    Exeter Rugby Club, founded in 1871, has a rich history, playing its first match in 1873 and winning the Devon Cup in 1890. Notably, in 1905, they hosted the first match played by New Zealand on English soil, a game that led to the "All Blacks" moniker.

    Early League and Professional Era

    Exeter made strides in the early '90s, reaching the Pilkington Cup quarter-finals in 1993 and 1995. They entered the Premiership Two in 1997, consistently finishing in the top half. In 2009, Exeter secured promotion to the Premiership, marking their debut season with an eighth-place finish and a quarter-final appearance in the European Challenge Cup.

    Premiership Success

    Exeter's Premiership journey saw them finish fifth in 2010-11, allowing entry into the Heineken Cup for the first time. In 2014, they secured their first major trophy, winning the Anglo-Welsh Cup. The 2016-17 season marked historic achievements, with Exeter winning the Premiership for the first time and reaching the European Champions Cup quarter-finals.

    Recent Success and Double Triumph

    The 2019-20 season was a pinnacle for Exeter as they achieved an unprecedented double, winning both the European Rugby Champions Cup and the Premiership. Despite reaching the Premiership final in 2017-18 and finishing eight points clear at the top in the regular season, they faced defeat against Saracens in the final.

    Logo Controversy and Change

    In 1999, Exeter turned semi-professional, adopting the name Exeter Chiefs and a Native American-themed logo. Over the years, controversy arose regarding cultural appropriation. In 2022, the club addressed concerns, retiring their mascot Big Chief and adopting imagery depicting the Iron Age tribe, the Dumnonii, in July.

    Logo Issue Resolution

    Amidst controversy over their Native American imagery, Exeter Chiefs made a significant decision in January 2022 to drop the branding, opting for imagery representing the Dumnonii tribe, addressing concerns about cultural sensitivity.
