    • About The Bulls

    The Bulls rugby team, based in Pretoria, South Africa, stands as a powerhouse in the country's rugby landscape. Established in 1938, the team has a rich history marked by numerous successes. The Bulls have claimed multiple titles in domestic competitions, including the Currie Cup and Vodacom Super Rugby. Notably, the mid-2000s marked a golden era for the Bulls, clinching three Super Rugby championships under the coaching leadership of Heyneke Meyer. Known for their formidable forward pack and dynamic backline, the Bulls continue to be a force on the rugby field. As stalwarts at Loftus Versfeld Stadium, the team enjoys unwavering support from passionate fans in Pretoria and beyond, cementing their status as one of South Africa's most celebrated rugby franchises.

    Early Super Rugby Ventures

    Before the Super Rugby era, Northern Transvaal made their mark in the Super 10 tournaments from 1993 to 1995. Despite a solid performance in the 1993 season, finishing third in their pool, they missed subsequent qualifications. The transition to the professional era brought about significant changes, leading to the creation of the Super 12 in 1996, where Northern Transvaal emerged as one of the formidable teams, securing a third-place finish.

    Building Foundations (1996–2005)

    In the inaugural Super 12 season (1996), Northern Transvaal showcased their prowess, finishing third on the table but faced a crushing defeat in the semi-final against the Auckland Blues. Despite the challenges, they continued to compete in subsequent seasons, gradually finding their footing. The franchise system was adopted in 1998, leading to the birth of the Bulls. Initial struggles persisted, but by 2003, the Bulls secured a semi-final berth, setting the stage for a period of transformation and growth.

    Super 14 Peaks (2006–2010)

    With the expansion to Super 14 in 2006, the Bulls faced intensified competition. A pivotal moment came in 2007 when they secured a historic 92-3 victory over the Queensland Reds, setting a new Super Rugby record. This momentum carried them to the semi-finals, where they fell to the Crusaders. The turning point arrived in 2009, with the Bulls clinching the Super 14 title, a feat they repeated in 2010, securing their place among rugby elites.

    Super Rugby Challenges (2011–2020)

    The Bulls faced formidable challenges in the Super Rugby era, with notable successes in 2009 and 2010. Despite achieving the South African Conference trophy in 2013, playoff matches proved elusive. The 2020 season brought an unprecedented pause due to the COVID-19 pandemic, leading to the creation of Super Rugby Unlocked, where the Bulls were crowned champions. The following years saw the Bulls adapting to new competitions, marking a dynamic phase in their rugby journey.

    Pro14 and United Rugby Championship Transition (2021–present)

    The Bulls embraced a new chapter in 2021, joining the Pro14 and later the United Rugby Championship. While facing initial challenges in Europe, they secured a home quarter-final in the 2021–22 season, showcasing resilience and determination as they continue to navigate the ever-evolving landscape of international rugby.
