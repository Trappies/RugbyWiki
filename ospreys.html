    • About The Ospreys

    Founded in 2003, the Ospreys are a Welsh professional rugby union team, competing at the pinnacle of European and domestic rugby. Based in Swansea, Wales, the team represents a collaboration between four Welsh rugby regions: Neath, Swansea, Bridgend, and Ospreylia. The Ospreys quickly asserted themselves as a powerhouse in the sport, winning the Celtic League in their debut season. Over the years, they have consistently been a force to be reckoned with in both the United Rugby Championship and European competitions. Boasting a strong roster of Welsh international players, the Ospreys embody the rich rugby tradition of Wales, captivating fans with their skill, passion, and commitment to the game. As they continue to chase glory on the field, the Ospreys remain a symbol of Welsh rugby pride.

    Infancy (2003-2004)

    In July 2003, the Neath–Swansea Ospreys emerged as a union between Neath RFC and Swansea RFC, led by coach Lyn Jones. Their first competitive game in September 2003 marked a Celtic League win over Ulster. Despite struggles in the Heineken Cup, domestically, they salvaged pride, finishing above Cardiff Blues in the Celtic League.

    Expansion and First Triumphs (2004-2007)

    Post the Celtic Warriors' demise in 2004, Ospreys extended their borders and dropped the "Neath–Swansea" from their name. The 2004–05 season witnessed their first silverware, clinching the Celtic League. Subsequent seasons saw ups and downs, with a notable win over the Wallabies in 2006, popularizing the term "Ospreylia."

    Continued Success and International Recognition (2008-2011)

    The 2006/07 season brought another Celtic League victory, making Ospreys the first team to win it twice. In 2008, they contributed 13 players to Wales' Six Nations match against England, setting a record. The following years included reaching the Heineken Cup quarter-final, British & Irish Lions selections, and another Celtic League triumph in 2009–10.

    Leadership Changes and Challenges (2012-2018)

    Steve Tandy took over as head coach in 2012, leading the team to a Pro12 victory in his first full season. Despite reaching two semi-finals in subsequent years, Ospreys faced challenges. Tandy's dismissal in 2018 marked a transitional period, with Allen Clarke taking interim charge.

    2018 Onwards: Revamp and Rejuvenation

    With Allen Clarke as head coach for the 2018–2019 season, Ospreys underwent significant changes. New signings, including Welsh internationals George North, Scott Williams, and Aled Davies, signaled a revamp. The season showed improvement, with 12 wins and notable victories over rivals like the Scarlets.
