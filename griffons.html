    • About The Griffons

    The Griffons, a formidable force in South African rugby, proudly represent the Northen Free State province in the annual Currie Cup tournament. Established to nurture rugby prowess in the heart of the country, the team draws its strength from a rich rugby heritage. The iconic Griffons emblem adorns their jerseys, signifying resilience and determination. The team's home ground, the North West Stadium in Welkom, resonates with the cheers of dedicated supporters. With a commitment to excellence and a passion for the sport, the Griffons continue to contribute to the dynamic tapestry of South African rugby, engaging in spirited competitions and showcasing the indomitable spirit that defines their legacy.

    Novavit Griffons: The Pride of Northern Free State

    The Novavit Griffons, formerly known as Northern Free State, stand as a formidable South African rugby union team in the annual Currie Cup and Rugby Challenge tournaments. Hailing from Welkom and calling the North West Stadium their fortress, the Griffons draw their strength from the vibrant rugby culture of the eastern third of the Free State Province. With a rich history dating back to 1968, the team was envisioned by the late Dr Danie Craven, reflecting a commitment to spreading the joy of rugby to rural areas.

    Home Turf Glory: North West Stadium in Welkom

    The North West Stadium in Welkom serves as the vibrant battleground for the Novavit Griffons. With a seating capacity that echoes with the cheers of approximately 4,000 passionate fans, this stadium becomes the heart of rugby action during the Currie Cup. As the Griffons showcase their prowess on home turf, the stadium transforms into a cauldron of excitement, witnessing thrilling encounters and memorable moments.

    Selection Honor: Path to the Cheetahs Super Rugby Team

    Players donning the Novavit Griffons jersey not only represent the pride of the team but also carry the honor of potential selection to the prestigious Cheetahs Super Rugby team. The pathway to the top echelons of South African rugby is paved through the dedication and skill displayed in Griffons colors. This interconnected journey fosters a strong rugby community and fuels the dreams of aspiring players.

    Rivalry Chronicles: Clash with Free State Cheetahs and NW Leopards

    The Novavit Griffons engage in intense battles with traditional rivals, the Free State Cheetahs, and the North West Province Leopards. These encounters, etched in the annals of South African rugby history, add spice to the competitive landscape. The matchups against familiar foes showcase the grit and determination that define the spirit of the Griffons.

    Evolution of Identity: From Northern Free State to Griffons

    Founded in 1968 as part of a visionary initiative by Dr Danie Craven, the team initially carried the moniker Northern Free State. However, with the advent of professional rugby in 1999, the team underwent a transformation, adopting the name Griffons. This evolution reflects the team's commitment to progress, resilience, and embracing a new era in the dynamic world of South African rugby.
