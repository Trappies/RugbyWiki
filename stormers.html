    • About The Stormers

    The Stormers, based in Cape Town, South Africa, is a powerhouse in the world of rugby. Competing in the United Rugby Championship and previously in Super Rugby, the team boasts a rich history and a formidable reputation. The Stormers have consistently been a force to be reckoned with, showcasing their skills and determination on the field. The team's iconic blue and white jerseys symbolize their commitment to excellence. With a strong fan base and a tradition of producing top-tier rugby talent, the Stormers continue to be a source of pride for South African rugby enthusiasts, embodying the spirit and resilience of the sport.

    DHL Stormers - Dominance in Southern Hemisphere Rugby

    The DHL Stormers, formerly known simply as the Stormers, are a South African rugby union team based in Cape Town. Competing in the United Rugby Championship, they were a formidable force in Super Rugby until 2020. The team draws players from the Western Province Currie Cup side, showcasing a rich history rooted in the Currie Cup. Despite early struggles in Super 12, the Stormers reached new heights in 1999, making the semi-finals for the first time. Their journey continued with notable seasons in 2004, 2010, and 2012, consistently showcasing their prowess. In the inaugural season of the United Rugby Championship (URC) in 2021–22, the Stormers clinched victory, securing the South African shield and the overall URC title. This success marked their transition to European competition in the European Rugby Champions Cup.

    Evolution of the Stormers in Super Rugby

    The Stormers' Super Rugby journey began in 1996 when the top Currie Cup sides gained entry. After a challenging start, they found success in 1999, reaching the semi-finals. The early 2000s saw fluctuations in performance, with highs in 2004 and lows in subsequent seasons. Rassie Erasmus's appointment in 2008 sparked a revival, and the team consistently improved. The memorable 2010 season, led by coach Allister Coetzee, saw the Stormers reach the final but fall to the Bulls. Despite continued success, including topping the log in 2012, they faced challenges in subsequent years, highlighting the competitive nature of Super Rugby.

    Farewell to Newlands - The Class of 2020

    In 2020, the Stormers bid farewell to the historic Newlands Rugby Stadium, marking the end of an era. Under the guidance of coach John Dobson and boasting a forward pack with World Cup-winning Springboks, the team aimed to leave a lasting legacy. The 2020 season represented a poignant farewell, and the class of 2020 sought to add long-awaited silverware as a tribute to the loyal supporters at Newlands. This transition set the stage for the Stormers' future endeavors in the evolving landscape of rugby competition.
