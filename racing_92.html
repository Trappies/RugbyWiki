    • About Racing 92

    Racing 92, based in the Paris suburb of Colombes, stands as one of France's premier rugby clubs, boasting a rich history and a formidable presence in European competitions. Originally founded in 1882, the club underwent several mergers and relocations before settling in its current form. Racing 92 has consistently been a contender in the Top 14, the French domestic league, and has left an indelible mark on the European stage. Their home matches at the impressive U Arena provide an electrifying atmosphere for fans, and the team's distinctive sky-blue and white jerseys are synonymous with a legacy of skillful and dynamic rugby. With a roster featuring international stars and a commitment to attacking flair, Racing 92 continues to be a powerhouse in French and European rugby, captivating audiences with their blend of tradition, innovation, and sporting excellence.

    A Legacy of Triumphs and Tradition

    Racing 92, a distinguished French professional rugby union club, traces its roots back to 1882 as a part of the Paris sports club Racing Club de France. The club's rich history includes numerous successes, with six league titles, one Pro D2 title, and a Coupe de l'Espérance. Renowned for its sky blue and white hooped home kit, Racing 92 is a powerhouse in the Top 14, competing at the impressive Paris La Défense Arena. The club's journey, marked by mergers and rebranding, led to its current form in 2001. Notably, Racing 92 clinched the Bouclier de Brennus in 2016 and consistently contends in the Top 14 playoffs. The team's move to the Paris La Défense Arena in 2017 symbolized a new era, and Racing 92 continues to captivate fans with its dynamic and ambitious approach to the game.

    Clash of Titans - Racing 92 vs. Stade Français

    Racing 92, one of France's oldest rugby clubs, has a storied history intertwined with a fierce rivalry with nearby club Stade Français. This rivalry dates back to the early years of French rugby, with both clubs contesting championship games in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. The intense competition reached its peak in 1892 when Racing secured the Bouclier de Brennus, setting the stage for a rivalry that would endure for decades. Despite facing ups and downs, Racing 92's clashes with Stade Français remain iconic moments in French rugby history, showcasing the passion and competitive spirit that define these two titans of the sport.

    From Decline to Dominance - Racing 92's Resilient Journey

    Racing 92's journey in the professional era reflects a tale of resilience and resurgence. Following a historic championship win in 1959, the club faced challenges adapting to professionalism, leading to relegation in 1996. The subsequent decades saw a rollercoaster of promotions and relegations. In 2001, the rugby section merged with US Métro, forming the current entity known as Racing Métro 92. Under the leadership of President Jacky Lorenzetti, Racing 92 embarked on a mission to reach the Top 14 and the Heineken Cup. Despite some setbacks, the club achieved these goals, rebranding to Racing 92 in 2015. Today, Racing 92 stands as a testament to perseverance and ambition in the ever-evolving landscape of professional rugby.
