    • About Toulouse

    Stade Toulousain, established in 1907, stands as one of the most successful and storied rugby clubs in France. Based in Toulouse, the "Red and Blacks" boast an illustrious history marked by a record-breaking 20 French championship titles and four European Rugby Champions Cup victories. The team has been a dominant force, producing some of the finest rugby talents and contributing significantly to the French national team. Stade Toulousain's home matches are played at the iconic Stadium de Toulouse, creating an electric atmosphere for fans. The club's commitment to excellence and its rich legacy make Stade Toulousain a true powerhouse in both French and European rugby.

    Roots and Foundation: A Heritage of Tradition and Unity

    Stade Toulousain's rich legacy traces back to its inception in 1907, a union between "Stade Olympien des Étudiants de Toulouse" and "Véto-Sport." The club's crest, influenced by Thomas Aquinas and the Basilica of Saint-Sernin, reflects the deep historical ties to the city. The iconic red and black colors, drawn from the ceremonial costume of the capitouls of Toulouse, signify a connection to the city's consuls. This merger of entities in 1907 laid the foundation for a rugby powerhouse that embodies the spirit and history of Toulouse.

    Early Years: Pioneers of French Rugby

    Stade Toulousain ventured into national rugby prominence in 1909, playing its first French Championship final and winning its inaugural title in 1912. The 1920s marked a golden era, securing championships in 1922, 1923, 1924, 1926, and 1927. Despite quieter decades, the club experienced a resurgence in the late 1940s, claiming the championship in 1947. The late 1980s mirrored the 1920s success, with back-to-back championships in 1985 and 1986, and victories in the Challenge Yves du Manoir.

    1970s to 1980s: Ups and Downs on the Championship Path

    The 1970s and 1980s brought mixed fortunes, with losses in finals against US Dax in 1971 and AS Béziers in 1980. However, the latter part of the decade witnessed a triumphant return, marked by consecutive championships in 1985, 1986, and 1989. The club also clinched the Challenge Yves du Manoir in 1988, showcasing a resurgence reminiscent of its earlier dominance.

    1990s to Present: The Era of Dominance and European Glory

    The 1990s marked a resurgence for Stade Toulousain, securing four consecutive championships from 1994 to 1997 and claiming the European Rugby Cup in 1996. The club continued its success in the 2000s, winning two more European titles in 2003 and 2005. Toulouse's triumphs extended into the late 2000s, with a historic fourth Heineken Cup title in 2010, making them the first club to achieve this feat. Recent successes include the 2019 French Rugby Union Championship, the historic 2021 Champions Cup, and the 2021 Top 14, showcasing Stade Toulousain's enduring legacy and continued dominance in French and European rugby.
