    • About Perpignan

    Perpignan Rugby, also known as USA Perpignan, is a distinguished French professional rugby union club based in Perpignan, Occitanie. Established in 1902, the club boasts a rich history and plays its home matches at the iconic Stade Aimé Giral, which has witnessed many thrilling rugby encounters. Sporting the distinctive colors of sang et or (blood and gold), Perpignan has been a force in French rugby, winning numerous accolades, including French top-division championships. The club has showcased resilience over the years, navigating various divisions and competitions, contributing significantly to the vibrant rugby culture in the region and beyond.

    Early Years

    Established in 1902 as AS Perpignan, the club's early successes culminated in the tragic loss of 19-year-old fly-half Aimé Giral during World War I. To honor Giral, the team adopted the colors of a Poilu uniform and named their stadium after him. The 1920s witnessed Perpignan's dominance, with multiple Championship victories.

    Mid-Century Triumphs

    Perpignan continued its success in the late 1930s, securing Championships and Challenge Yves du Manoir titles. The post-war era brought another Championship in 1944 and subsequent victories in 1955. The club maintained its prowess, winning the Challenge Yves du Manoir in 1965 and reaching the 1977 Championship final.

    Professional Era

    Entering the professional era, Perpignan faced challenges but remained a formidable force. In 2003, they reached the European final, and in 2009, they clinched the Top 14 Championship. The club's commitment to excellence was evident in signings like All Blacks fly-half Dan Carter.

    Recent Achievements

    Despite a brief stint with FC Barcelona and twinning agreements, Perpignan's focus remained on rugby glory. In 2010, they reached the Top 14 final again, showcasing their resilience. The club's legacy and commitment to success continue to resonate in the world of French rugby.
