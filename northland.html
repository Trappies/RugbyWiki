    • About Northland

    The Northland Rugby Union, based in Whangarei, New Zealand, has been a formidable force in the country's rugby landscape. Established in 1920, the union has contributed significantly to the sport's legacy. Competing in the Mitre 10 Cup, Northland's home games resonate with the passionate support of their fans. The team has witnessed both challenges and triumphs over the years, showcasing the resilience and skill that define New Zealand rugby. With notable players emerging from their ranks and a commitment to the game's values, Northland continues to play a vital role in the rich tapestry of New Zealand rugby.

    Northland Taniwha Legacy

    Founded in 1920, the Northland Taniwha rugby union team boasts a rich history that spans over a century. As a stalwart in New Zealand rugby, they have evolved with the changing landscape of the sport, establishing themselves as a force to be reckoned with.

    Home at Semenoff Stadium

    Semenoff Stadium in Whangārei serves as the fortress for the Northland Taniwha. The iconic venue resonates with the cheers of passionate fans as the team showcases their skills on the field. It's more than just a stadium; it's the heart of Northland rugby.

    National Provincial Championship Contenders

    Stepping onto the field in the reformed National Provincial Championship, the Northland Taniwha bring their A-game. With a commitment to excellence and a roster of skilled players, they consistently challenge opponents and aim for supremacy in New Zealand's premier provincial competition.

    Affiliation with Blues Super Rugby

    Proudly affiliated with the Blues Super Rugby franchise, the Northland Taniwha serve as a breeding ground for talent that transitions to the elite level. This collaboration reinforces their role in shaping the future of rugby in New Zealand.

    Sky Blue Pride

    Draped in sky blue, the Northland Taniwha don their iconic colors with pride. Beyond being a symbol of unity on the field, these colors represent a tradition of excellence and a commitment to upholding the values that define the essence of New Zealand rugby.
