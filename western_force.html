    • About The Western Force

    The Western Force, based in Perth, Australia, emerged in 2006 as one of the expansion teams in the Super 14 competition. Established to represent the Western Australia region, the Force quickly gained attention for their competitive spirit. The inaugural season showed promise, and in subsequent years, the team became a formidable presence in Australian rugby. Despite facing financial challenges and uncertainties about their future in the Super Rugby competition, the Force continued to showcase their resilience on the field. After a brief hiatus, the team returned to Super Rugby in 2020 for the Australian domestic competition, Super Rugby AU, and has since been a determined force in the rugby landscape, embodying the spirit of Western Australian rugby with pride.

    Inception and Triumph: The Western Force Bid

    In the early 2000s, the Western Force embarked on a groundbreaking journey to secure entry into an expanded Super 12 competition. Spearheaded by RugbyWA, the bid gained momentum in 2002 when a dedicated group was formed to oversee the developments. Backed by the state government and armed with a million-dollar loan, RugbyWA successfully outbid Rugby Union Victoria, securing the rights to host the fourth Australian franchise in December 2004. This marked the beginning of an era for Western Australian rugby.

    Debut Challenges and Milestones

    The Western Force made their eagerly awaited debut in the 2006 Super 14 season, facing the Brumbies in front of a near sold-out Subiaco Oval in Perth. Despite a challenging start, including losses to the Hurricanes and Chiefs, the Force earned their first competition point in a hard-fought match against the Stormers. The debut season featured memorable moments, including a remarkable draw with the undefeated Crusaders and significant signings like Matt Giteau. The Force's inaugural win against the Cheetahs in May 2006 marked a historic achievement for the team.

    Super Rugby Years: Challenges and Farewell

    The Western Force continued to compete in Super Rugby until the 2017 season, facing challenges and striving for success. Despite their best efforts, they never qualified for the playoffs during this period. The turning point came in 2017 when SANZAAR decided to reduce the competition from 18 to 15 teams, leading to the Force's unfortunate exclusion from Super Rugby. This marked the end of an era, but it paved the way for new opportunities and challenges.

    Global Rapid Rugby and Resilience

    Following their Super Rugby departure, the Western Force embraced new opportunities, particularly in Global Rapid Rugby and the National Rugby Championship. Under the leadership of Australian billionaire Andrew Forrest, the team participated in the inaugural Global Rapid Rugby season, showcasing their resilience and commitment to the sport. Simultaneously, they excelled in the National Rugby Championship, winning the 2019 season and reinforcing their presence in the rugby landscape.

    Return to Super Rugby: Navigating Challenges

    The unexpected twists brought by the COVID-19 pandemic prompted the Western Force's return to Super Rugby in 2020, competing in the inaugural Super Rugby AU season. Despite the challenges posed by the pandemic, the Force demonstrated their adaptability and determination. They continued their journey into the 2021 Super Rugby AU season and Super Rugby Trans-Tasman, showcasing their prowess on the field.

    Uncertain Future and Triumphant Return

    As the future of Super Rugby became uncertain, a new 12-team competition emerged in 2022. The Western Force, now a full-time participant, competed against Australian and New Zealand teams, along with Fijian Drua and Moana Pasifika. Despite narrowly missing the Finals Series in the inaugural season, the Force's triumphant return solidified their status as a force to be reckoned with in the ever-evolving world of rugby.
