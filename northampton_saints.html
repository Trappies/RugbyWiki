    • About The Northampton Saints

    The Northampton Saints, based in Northampton, England, stand as one of the premier rugby clubs in the Gallagher Premiership. Founded in 1880, the Saints boast a rich history and a passionate fan base. Franklin's Gardens serves as their iconic home ground, providing a vibrant atmosphere for thrilling rugby encounters. The team, donned in black, green, and gold, has seen notable successes, including capturing the Premiership title and European Challenge Cup. With a commitment to developing local talent and attracting international stars, the Northampton Saints continue to be a formidable force in English rugby, known for their skillful play and tenacious spirit on the field.

    Early Years: Establishing The Saints Legacy (1880-1995)

    Founded in 1880 as Northampton St. James, the club aimed to instill order and discipline among the youth through rugby. Reverend Samuel Wathen Wigg, the founder, set the foundation for a club that would evolve into a rugby powerhouse. In the early 20th century, the iconic figure of Edgar Mobbs emerged, becoming the first Northampton player to captain England but tragically lost his life in World War I. The post-war period saw the Saints flourishing, producing top-tier players who went on to captain their country. However, challenges arose as the club struggled to keep up with the changing rugby landscape, leading to the 1988 'Saints Revolution.' Barrie Corless and All Blacks legend Buck Shelford played pivotal roles in revitalizing the club, setting the stage for a resurgence.

    Professional Era: Triumphs and Challenges (1995-2007)

    The transition to professionalism in 1995 marked a new era for the Saints, with local businessman Keith Barwell taking over. Under the leadership of directors like Ian McGeechan, the Saints reached new heights, winning the European Cup in 2000 and undergoing a transformative period known as the "Demolition Tour of Division Two" in 1995-96. The club continued its success in the following years, with notable achievements in the Anglo-Welsh Cup and Heineken Cup. However, the 2007-08 season brought a setback as Northampton faced relegation from the Premiership. The subsequent restructuring, led by Jim Mallinder and Dorian West, saw the Saints reclaim their place in the top flight, securing promotion and winning the EDF Energy Trophy.

    Return to Premiership and Recent Success (2008-2018)

    The return to the Premiership in 2008 marked the beginning of a new chapter for the Saints. Season after season, they demonstrated their prowess, finishing eighth in 2008-09 and clinching the European Challenge Cup in 2009. Success continued with victories in the Anglo-Welsh Cup and a strong performance in the English Premiership. The 2013-14 season stands out as one of the club's most successful, winning the English Premiership and the European Challenge Cup. However, challenges emerged in the following seasons, leading to the departure of director of rugby Jim Mallinder in 2017. A period of rebuilding ensued, culminating in the appointment of Hurricanes boss Chris Boyd in 2018, signaling a new era for the Northampton Saints.

    2018 Onwards: A New Dawn with Chris Boyd

    The arrival of Chris Boyd in 2018 marked a turning point for the Saints. Boyd, with a successful track record, brought a fresh perspective and leadership. In his inaugural season, the Saints secured the Premiership Rugby Cup, showcasing their resurgence. The team achieved a top-four finish, signaling a return to prominence. Boyd's tenure breathed new life into the club, and the Saints embarked on a journey of rediscovery and success, laying the groundwork for a promising future in the highly competitive world of rugby.
