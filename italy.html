    • About Italy Rugby

    Italy entered the world of Test rugby in 1929, marking the beginning of a journey in international rugby competition. Although the Azzurri faced initial challenges, their presence in the Rugby World Cup commenced in 1987. Over the years, Italy has seen notable moments in the tournament, showcasing their determination on the global stage. In the professional era, Italy has experienced highs and lows, with standout achievements including victories in the Six Nations and notable performances against rugby powerhouses. The Azzurri continue to evolve, and their journey in international rugby reflects a commitment to the sport's growth and competitiveness.

    Early Years: 1911–34

    Italy's entry into Test rugby in 1911 marked the beginning of a journey. The "Propaganda Committee" formed in 1911, evolving into the Federazione Italiana Rugby (FIR) in 1928. The inaugural international match in 1929 against Spain set the stage for Italy's participation in the founding of FIRA (Rugby Europe) in 1934, alongside nations like France, Spain, and Romania.

    1945–85

    World War II posed a hiatus for Italian rugby. Post-war reconstruction, aided by Allied troops, led to a resurgence. The 1970s and 1980s witnessed significant progress with the influence of foreign players and coaches, including notable victories against Argentina in 1978.

    1986–99

    Italy aspired to join an expanded Five Nations Championship, showcasing their potential. They consistently defeated nations like Romania and Spain. The late 1980s marked their entry into the Rugby World Cup, facing formidable opponents like New Zealand. The 1990s brought memorable wins over Ireland, France, and Scotland, setting the stage for their inclusion in the Six Nations in 2000.

    Six Nations era: 2000–present

    Italy's debut in the Six Nations Championship in 2000 saw an initial victory against Scotland. Despite challenges, they recorded notable wins over Wales in 2003 and Scotland in 2004. The mid-2000s saw Italy build a formidable side, achieving consecutive victories in the Six Nations and gaining respect on the international stage.

    Recent Challenges: 2010–2021

    Italy faced ups and downs in the Six Nations, occasionally finishing at the bottom but also recording historic wins, such as defeating France in 2013. The years 2016 to 2021 witnessed a tough period, marked by consecutive losses in the Six Nations.

    Triumphs in 2022

    The historic win over Wales in the final match of the 2022 Six Nations Championship marked a turning point for Italy. This victory, coupled with successful performances in the autumn tour, including victories over Samoa and Australia, showcased Italy's resilience and progress. The triumph signaled a new chapter for Italian rugby, hinting at a promising future on the international stage.
