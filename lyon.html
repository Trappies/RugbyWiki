    • About Lyon

    The Lyon Rugby Team, known as Lyon OU (Olympique Universitaire), is a prominent French rugby club based in Lyon. Established in 1896, the club has a rich history in French rugby. Lyon OU competes in the top-tier domestic league, the Top 14, showcasing their prowess in the highly competitive French rugby landscape. Over the years, the team has been a formidable presence, with a strong fan base and a commitment to developing top-tier talent. Lyon OU's matches are played at the Matmut Stadium de Gerland, a venue steeped in rugby tradition. The club's colors, red and black, symbolize its identity and spirit. Lyon OU continues to be a significant force in French rugby, contributing to the nation's vibrant rugby culture and tradition.

    Lyon OU's Promotion History

    Lyon Olympique Universitaire Rugby, commonly known as LOU, has a dynamic history of promotions and relegations in recent years. Founded in 1896, the club has moved between the Top 14 and Pro D2 levels. Notable promotions occurred in 2011, 2014, and 2016, while facing relegations in 2012 and 2015. This rollercoaster journey adds an intriguing dimension to LOU's modern era in French rugby.

    Evolution of LOU's Identity

    Established in 1896 as Racing Club, Lyon Olympique Universitaire Rugby underwent several name changes, evolving into its current identity. The club's original name resulted from the merger of Racing Club de Vaise and Rugby Club de Lyon. The transformation continued, leading to the adoption of the name Lyon Olympique Universitaire in 1910. The distinctive red and black colors were embraced in 1902, contributing to LOU's enduring identity in French rugby.

    Historic Rugby Achievements

    Lyon Olympique Universitaire Rugby, known as Le LOU, holds a significant place in French rugby history. One of the oldest sports clubs in the country, Le LOU participated in three consecutive French championship finals from 1931 to 1933. While losing the first final to Toulon, they secured victories in the subsequent two finals against Narbonne. Despite later experiencing periods in lower amateur leagues, their rich history adds prestige to the club's legacy.

    Venue Transitions: Stade Vuillermet to Matmut Stadium de Gerland

    In 2011, Lyon Olympique Universitaire Rugby bid farewell to the Stade Vuillermet and embraced a new era at the Matmut Stadium. This shift marked a significant chapter in the club's history. In 2017, they made another move to the Matmut Stadium de Gerland, solidifying their presence in modern rugby facilities and contributing to the evolving landscape of French rugby venues.

    LOU's Ambitious Era (2006-2007)

    During the 2006-2007 season, Lyon Olympique Universitaire Rugby displayed ambitious aspirations, boasting the second-largest budget in the championship. Under the leadership of coach Christian Lanta, who had previously guided Racing Club de France, Italian club Treviso, and Agen, LOU aimed to rejoin the Top 14 within the next two years. Despite the ambitious plans, the promotion was achieved in 2011, marking the beginning of LOU's "yo-yo" trajectory between top-tier and second-level rugby.
