    • About The Springboks

    South Africa first played Test rugby in 1891, against a British Isles touring team. The Springboks made their Rugby World Cup debut in 1995, when the newly democratic South Africa hosted the tournament and the team, under the captaincy of Francois Pienaar, defeated the All Blacks in the final (15-12), a moment which is remembered as one of the greatest in SA's sporting history. The Springboks also won the Rugby World Cup in France in 2007, with John Smit as captain, when they beat England in the final in Paris by 15-6. South Africa then lifted the Webb Ellis Cup for a third time in 2019, when the Boks, under Siya Kolisi as captain, again beat England in the final in Tokyo, by 32-12. Four years later, in Paris, Kolisi again led the Boks to the RWC title as they overcame NZ by 12-11. Other highlights of the professional era include victories in the Rugby Championship (Tri-Nations) in 1998, 2004, 2009 and 2019, as well as series victories over the British & Irish Lions in 2009 and 2021.

    Early Years: 1891–1913

    The Springboks' inaugural international matches and the rise of British Isles dominance. From a winless start in 1891 to South Africa's first test victory in 1903, the team's trajectory was set for future glory.

    European Tour Triumphs: 1906–1913

    The birth of the "Springboks" nickname during the 1906 tour, followed by the first Grand Slam in 1912–13, solidifying South Africa's rugby prowess on European soil.

    Between Wars: 1920s–1930s

    Iconic battles against New Zealand and the emergence of unique tactics. The 1930s saw victories over Australia and New Zealand, setting the stage for South Africa's second Grand Slam in 1931–32.

    Global Touring: 1930s–1940s

    Triumphs against Australia, New Zealand, and Europe. The Springboks asserted dominance in series wins and international tours, leaving an indelible mark on rugby history.

    Anti-apartheid Protests: 1960s–1970s

    Growing international criticism amid apartheid, marked by victories and challenges. The 1960s and '70s witnessed protests, cancellations, and a changing landscape for the Springboks.

    Sporting Isolation: 1970s–1980s

    The Gleneagles Agreement impact and the Cavaliers tour. South Africa faced isolation, rebel tours, and political turmoil during a period of intense global scrutiny.

    Rainbow Nation and 1995 World Cup

    The end of apartheid, readmission to international rugby, and the triumph of the iconic 1995 Rugby World Cup victory. A historic moment uniting a nation.

    Post-Millennium Struggles: 2000s–2010s

    Challenges in the new millennium, including record defeats and a revival in 2004. The highs of winning Tri-Nations and lows of the 2003 World Cup quarter-final exit.

    Erasmus/Kolisi Era: 2018–present

    Rassie Erasmus leads a revival, marked by historic wins, Tri-Nations success, and Siya Kolisi's captaincy. The team's resilience and commitment define a new era.

    2019 Rugby World Cup Triumph

    A remarkable journey culminating in a World Cup victory against England in 2019. Siya Kolisi, the first black South African captain, lifts the Webb Ellis Cup, symbolizing unity.

    Navigating the Pandemic: 2020–2021

    Facing disruptions during the COVID-19 pandemic, the Springboks adapt to new challenges, withdrawing from the Rugby Championship and making a triumphant return in 2021.

    2023 Rugby World Cup Glory

    Securing a consecutive World Cup win, defeating New Zealand in a nail-biting final. The Springboks' fourth title cements their legacy as one of rugby's greatest teams.
