    • About French Rugby

    Rugby has a rich history in France, dating back to the late 19th century. The sport was introduced to the country by British expatriates, and its popularity quickly grew. The French Rugby Federation (FFR) was founded in 1919, solidifying the organization of the sport in the country. France has been a prominent force in international rugby, participating in the inaugural Five Nations Championship (now Six Nations) in 1910. The national team, known as Les Bleus, has experienced both triumphs and challenges over the years, capturing the Grand Slam in the Six Nations and reaching the Rugby World Cup final multiple times. The dynamic and passionate rugby culture in France continues to evolve, making the country a significant player in the global rugby community.

    Early Roots: 1872–1906

    French rugby traces its origins back to 1872 when English merchants and students introduced the sport to the country. The formal recognition came on February 26, 1890, when a team from Janson Desailly Lyceum achieved victory in the Bois de Boulogne. Despite early representation at the 1900 Summer Olympics, France's first official test match occurred on New Year's Day in 1906 against the New Zealand All Blacks in Paris, marking the beginning of a storied rugby journey.

    Interwar Challenges and Triumphs: 1910–1939

    France faced intermittent matches against the Home Nations until 1910 when they joined to form the Five Nations tournament. However, in 1932, France was expelled from the competition due to accusations of professionalism in their leagues. Despite this setback, they went on an impressive winning streak from 1931 to 1936. The invitation to rejoin in 1939 was interrupted by World War II, with France resuming international play in 1947.

    The Golden Era: 1950s–1960s

    The 1950s and 1960s marked a turning point for French rugby. They secured their first Five Nations championship in 1954, shared with England and Wales, and clinched their first outright title in 1959. Notable victories on South African soil and successful tours of New Zealand and Australia highlighted France's rise. The pinnacle was reached in 1968 with their first Five Nations Grand Slam, beating all competing teams.

    Grand Slam Glory: 1970s–1980s

    France continued their success in the 1970s, securing their second Grand Slam in 1977. The momentum carried into the 1980s with two more Grand Slams in 1981 and 1987. The latter coincided with the inaugural Rugby World Cup, where France faced the All Blacks in the final, though falling short with a 29–9 defeat.

    Ups and Downs: 1990s–2000s

    The 1990s brought mixed fortunes, including a Grand Slam in 1997 and 1998 but a heartbreaking loss to the Wallabies in the 1999 World Cup final. The new millennium saw France expanding their rugby horizons, winning a Grand Slam in 2002 and achieving consistent success in the Six Nations Championship.

    World Cup Challenges and Triumphs: 2000s–2019

    France's journey in the Rugby World Cup involved reaching the final in 2011, losing narrowly to the All Blacks. Notable performances, including defeating the All Blacks in the 2007 quarterfinals, showcased France's competitive spirit. The 2019 World Cup witnessed a quarterfinal loss to Wales, marking a determined yet challenging campaign.

    Recent Glory: 2022 and Beyond

    In 2022, the French rugby team achieved a perfect season, winning all 13 matches. This recent success signals a promising era for French rugby, as they aim to build on their rich history and continue making an impact on the global stage.
