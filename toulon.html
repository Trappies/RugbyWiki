    • About Toulon

    Founded in 1908, RC Toulonnais, commonly known as Toulon Rugby, is a French rugby union club based in Toulon, Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur. The team boasts a rich history and has become a powerhouse in European rugby. Toulon has experienced significant success, particularly in recent years, winning three consecutive European Rugby Champions Cups from 2013 to 2015, solidifying their status as one of the continent's premier clubs. The team's home matches are played at the Stade Mayol, a venue renowned for its passionate atmosphere. Toulon has attracted top-tier international talent, contributing to their formidable roster. With a blend of tradition, success, and star-studded lineups, Toulon Rugby continues to be a force to be reckoned with in the world of professional rugby.

    Rugby Club Toulonnais - French Rugby Royalty

    Founded in 1908 and based in Toulon, Rugby Club Toulonnais, also known as Toulon, holds a storied history in French rugby. The club has secured four league titles, two Pro D2 titles, and two Challenge Yves du Manoir domestically. Internationally, Toulon achieved a remarkable feat by winning the Heineken Cup/European Rugby Champions Cup three times consecutively. The club, with a vibrant fan base, iconic red and black kits, and a stadium with a capacity of 17,500 at Stade Mayol, has etched its place in the hearts of rugby enthusiasts.

    Toulon's Resilience and Glory Through the Years

    Facing financial challenges in the early 2000s, Toulon's journey included relegation and subsequent promotion from the Second Division. The club, under the leadership of president Mourad Boudjellal, witnessed a transformative period marked by strategic signings, including legends like Jonny Wilkinson. Their return to the Top 14 in the 2008–09 season set the stage for remarkable achievements. Toulon's resurgence saw them finish second domestically, securing a spot in the Heineken Cup and showcasing their prowess on the European stage.

    Triumph in Diversity - Toulon's Star-Studded Lineup

    Mourad Boudjellal's vision for Toulon included assembling a diverse and star-studded lineup. Notable signings such as Tana Umaga, George Gregan, Andrew Mehrtens, and Jonny Wilkinson brought experience and international flair to the team. This strategic approach not only bolstered Toulon's presence in the Top 14 but also elevated them to become a formidable force in European competitions.

    Toulon's European Conquest

    At the pinnacle of their success, Toulon clinched the 2013 Heineken Cup Final, securing a narrow 16–15 victory against Clermont Auvergne. This triumph solidified their status as a European rugby powerhouse. The club's ability to navigate challenges, sign rugby legends, and deliver stellar performances on the pitch has left an indelible mark on the history of Rugby Club Toulonnais.
