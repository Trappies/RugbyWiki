    • About Western Province

    The Western Province Rugby team, based in Cape Town, South Africa, is synonymous with a legacy of excellence in the world of rugby. Established in 1883, the team boasts a rich history and a strong presence in South African rugby. They proudly wear blue and white hoops as their home playing colors, symbolizing the tradition and pride associated with the Western Province. Newlands Stadium in Cape Town serves as the hallowed ground where the team showcases its skill and determination. Affiliated with the Stormers Super Rugby franchise, the Western Province Rugby team continues to captivate fans with their dynamic style of play and unwavering commitment to success on the rugby field. As stalwarts of South African rugby, they carry the torch of tradition while forging new chapters of glory in each game they play.

    DHL Western Province Rugby: A Rich Tradition of Triumph

    DHL Western Province, based in Newlands, Cape Town, is a powerhouse in South African rugby with a legacy dating back to 1883. Recognized for sponsorship reasons as DHL Western Province, the team has clinched numerous titles, including an impressive 34 Currie Cup triumphs, Vodacom Cup victories, Absa Nite Series, and Lion Cup successes. The iconic blue and white hooped jerseys, known as Die Streeptruie, symbolize the team's legacy. In 2012, they secured the Vodacom Cup, defeating Griquas 2018, and claimed the Currie Cup in 2014 by overcoming the Lions 1916. Western Province stands as the only South African team, alongside the Blue Bulls, to feature in every final of various competitions, showcasing their enduring excellence in South African rugby. With historic victories over the All Blacks, Wallabies, and British and Irish Lions, DHL Western Province continues to shape the rugby landscape, fueled by a 30-year sponsorship with Adidas and unwavering fan support.

    Historical Dominance and Rivalry

    Established in 1883, Western Province's rich history includes dominating the Currie Cup in the 1890s and early 1900s. The team won titles in 1914, the 1920s, and the 1930s, creating a legacy that spans decades. A fierce rivalry with Northern Transvaal (now Blue Bulls) intensified during South Africa's sporting isolation, culminating in multiple championship finals. The 1980s marked Western Province's golden era, clinching five consecutive titles. Their reign continued with victories in 2000 and 2001, defeating the Sharks both times. Notably, the club's 2012 triumph in the Currie Cup and their 2014 success solidify Western Province's status as a dominant force in South African rugby. The club's remarkable achievements extend across Super Rugby, Vodacom Cup, and Currie Cup competitions, making them a true icon in the rugby realm.

    Unprecedented Success and Contemporary Glory

    In recent times, Western Province has demonstrated unparalleled success, making three consecutive Currie Cup finals and securing the title in 2014 against the Lions. The club's prowess extends to Super Rugby, where they clinched the Conference Cup thrice in five years, showcasing their dominance from 2011 to 2015. Their unbeaten run in the 2013 Currie Cup season further underscores their contemporary glory. With a strong fan base and enduring partnerships with sponsors like DHL and Adidas, Western Province remains a formidable force, shaping the narrative of South African rugby with each triumphant campaign.
