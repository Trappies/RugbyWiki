    • About Bay of Plenty

    The Bay of Plenty Rugby Union, established in 1911, governs rugby union in the Bay of Plenty region, New Zealand. Represented by the Bay of Plenty Steamers in the Mitre 10 Cup, the team has played a significant role in New Zealand's rugby landscape. The union's distinctive blue and gold colors symbolize its identity. Over the years, the Bay of Plenty Steamers has displayed competitiveness in the national competitions, contributing to the region's rugby legacy. The union has also been involved in Ranfurly Shield challenges, showcasing its ambition and commitment to New Zealand's rich rugby traditions. With a history spanning more than a century, the Bay of Plenty rugby team continues to be a source of pride for its supporters and a formidable competitor on the rugby field.

    Bay of Plenty Rugby Union Establishment

    The Bay of Plenty Rugby Union, founded in 1911, has a rich history in New Zealand rugby, establishing its presence well before the inception of the National Provincial Championship in 1976.

    Bay of Plenty Steamers in Professional Rugby

    Known as the Bay of Plenty Steamers, the team has evolved into a professional rugby force, competing in the reformed National Provincial Championship. Their commitment to the sport is evident in their affiliation with the Chiefs Super Rugby franchise.

    Home Grounds in the Bay of Plenty Region

    The Bay of Plenty Steamers showcase their prowess at Rotorua International Stadium in Rotorua and Tauranga Domain in Tauranga, both iconic venues in the Bay of Plenty Region, providing a vibrant and enthusiastic atmosphere for their home games.

    Affiliation with Chiefs Super Rugby

    As a proud member of the Chiefs Super Rugby franchise, the Bay of Plenty Steamers contribute to the wider rugby community, fostering a strong connection between regional and national levels of the sport.

    Striking Blue and Yellow Colors

    The team's identity is reflected in its distinctive blue and yellow colors, a powerful symbol that resonates with fans and underscores the Bay of Plenty's significance in New Zealand's rugby landscape.
