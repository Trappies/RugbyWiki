    • About Pau

    Pau Rugby, officially known as Section Paloise, is a distinguished French rugby union club with a storied legacy. Based in the picturesque city of Pau in southwestern France, the team has been a prominent fixture in French rugby since its establishment in 1902. Sporting green and white, the club has a rich history in both domestic and international competitions. Over the years, Pau Rugby has nurtured talented players and fostered a strong connection with its passionate fanbase. The club's commitment to excellence and its contributions to the vibrant rugby culture in France make Pau Rugby a respected and integral part of the nation's rugby landscape.

    Rugby Roots in Pau and Béarn

    Pau, the third major provincial French city to embrace rugby, witnessed the sport's emergence around 1890 with teams like Coquelicots de Pau engaging in matches against neighboring rivals. Stade Palois, founded in 1899 by Anglophile former students, transitioned from a Barette team to rugby union. The club's transformation culminated in its historic French championship win in 1928, defeating US Quillan 6-4 in a fiercely contested final.

    Title Triumph (1902 - 1939)

    Under Gilbert Pierrot's leadership, Section Paloise dominated the 1927-1928 season, clinching the Côte Basque title and triumphing in the French championship. The final showdown against US Quillan drew 30,000 spectators, witnessing a hard-fought 6-4 win for Section Paloise. This marked the club's first Bouclier de Brennus, sparking celebrations and solidifying its place in rugby history.

    Post-WWII Glory (1946)

    Section Paloise continued its success post-World War II, securing the French championship in 1946 by defeating FC Lourdes 11-0 at the Parc des Princes. Led by figures like Theo Cazenave, Pierre Lauga, and captain André Rousse, the team showcased remarkable cohesion and determination. The victory marked the club's third French championship title.

    Robert Paparemborde Era (1965 - 1990)

    In 1964, Section Paloise, with stars like Robert Paparemborde, claimed their third French championship, overcoming Béziers 13-0. Paparemborde's versatile talent from inside centre to prop solidified his status as a rugby icon. The team's success triggered jubilant celebrations, showcasing the unwavering support of Pau and Béarn.

    Revival and Fall (1990 - 2006)

    In 1990, Section Paloise shifted to Stade du Hameau, marking a new era. The 1998 season saw them reach the H-Cup semifinals, falling to English side Bath. Despite subsequent struggles, including relegation in 2006, the club's resilience laid the groundwork for future triumphs.

    Rebuilding and Ambitious Return (Since 2006)

    After earning promotion in 2011-2012 and securing Top 14 access in 2015, Section Paloise underwent a resurgence. Manager Simon Mannix's strategic recruitment, bringing in talents like Colin Slade, Carl Hayman, and Conrad Smith, aimed at elevating the team's performance. Although Mannix departed in 2019, the team, now coached by Sébastien Piqueronies, continues to evolve with a focus on sustained success.
