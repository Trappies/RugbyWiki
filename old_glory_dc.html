    • About Old Glory DC

    Old Glory DC is a professional rugby union team based in Washington, D.C. The team was established in 2018 and competes in Major League Rugby (MLR), the premier rugby competition in the United States. Old Glory DC made its debut in MLR during the 2020 season. The team plays its home matches at Segra Field, a multi-purpose stadium located in Leesburg, Virginia. Old Glory DC has rapidly become a prominent force in American rugby, contributing to the growth and popularity of the sport in the nation's capital and beyond.

    Old Glory DC RFC - Introduction

    Old Glory DC RFC is a distinguished professional rugby union team based in the Washington, D.C., area, proudly representing the region in Major League Rugby (MLR). Established in 2018 and led by former USA Eagles player Paul Sheehy and local rugby enthusiast Chris Dunlavey, the team officially entered regular-season MLR play in 2020.

    Ownership and Coaching Leadership

    Under the guidance of local business leaders Paul Sheehy and Chris Dunlavey, Old Glory DC RFC has gained a solid foundation in the rugby community. The Scottish Rugby Union also holds a part ownership stake in the team. The coaching helm has been entrusted to Simon Cross since July 2023, ensuring strategic direction and expertise on the field.

    History and Expansion

    Major League Rugby's expansion announcement on May 15, 2018, paved the way for Old Glory DC RFC to join the league by the 2020 season. The team's official confirmation followed in November 2018, and on February 6, 2019, the distinctive name "Old Glory DC" was unveiled. Notably, the Scottish Rugby Union acquired a minority interest in the team on March 28, 2019.

    Coaching Transitions and Achievements

    In response to a challenging start to the 2022 season, head coach Andrew Douglas departed in March 2022, marking the end of his tenure as the longest-serving head coach in MLR. Nate Osborne assumed the role of interim head coach until the appointment of Josh Syms on October 31, 2022. Despite Syms' departure in May 2023, Old Glory DC secured its first playoff berth, leading to the recruitment of Simon Cross as the new head coach on July 7, 2023.

    Home Venue Shift

    Old Glory DC RFC has called Segra Field in Leesburg, Virginia, its home from 2021 to 2023. Excitingly, a move to the Maryland SoccerPlex in Germantown, Maryland, was announced on September 6, 2023, promising a dynamic new setting for the team's 2024 season.
