    • About Toronto Arrows

    The Toronto Arrows are a professional rugby union team based in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. Competing in Major League Rugby (MLR), the team was founded in 2017 and joined the league for its inaugural 2018 season. Known for their distinctive maple leaf logo, the Arrows have quickly become a prominent force in North American rugby. The team plays its home games at Lamport Stadium, contributing to the growing popularity of rugby in Canada. The Arrows' commitment to fostering local talent and engaging their passionate fanbase has solidified their position as a key player in the MLR landscape.

    Toronto Arrows RFC in MLR

    The Toronto Arrows RFC, originally founded as the Ontario Arrows in 2017, played a vital role in Major League Rugby (MLR). Established with the aim of joining MLR in 2019, the team showcased its potential through an exhibition schedule and became an MLR franchise in November 2018, subsequently adopting the name Toronto Arrows. Led by Co-founder & President Bill Webb and a diverse ownership group, the team faced challenges during the COVID-19 pandemic, temporarily relocating to Marietta, Georgia, for the 2021 season. Despite these hurdles, the Arrows continued to play a significant role in MLR, hosting games in various locations, including British Columbia, and capturing the rugby community's support. Tragically, on August 30, 2023, the passing of President Bill Webb marked a somber moment for the team. Regrettably, on November 27, 2023, it was announced that the Toronto Arrows would not participate in the 2024 MLR season, signaling a hiatus for this influential rugby club.
