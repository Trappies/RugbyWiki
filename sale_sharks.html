    • About The Sale Sharks

    Sale Sharks, based in Greater Manchester, England, are a prominent professional rugby union club with a rich history in the English Premiership. Established in 1861, Sale has undergone significant transformations and emerged as a formidable force in domestic and European rugby. Known for their dynamic playing style and competitive spirit, the Sharks have achieved notable successes, including winning the Premiership title in the 2005-06 season. With a commitment to developing talent, Sale Sharks have produced top-tier players who have not only excelled at the club level but have also made significant contributions to international rugby. The AJ Bell Stadium serves as their home ground, providing a vibrant backdrop for fans to witness thrilling encounters. As Sale Sharks continue to evolve, their dedication to excellence and a strong rugby tradition remains central to their identity in the world of English rugby.

    1861–1990s: Historic Foundation and Dominance

    Sale Sharks, founded in 1861, boast a rich legacy as one of England's oldest rugby clubs. In 1911, they achieved an impressive unbeaten streak of 26 matches. The 1930s marked a dominant period with players like Hal Sever, Claude Davey, and Wilf Wooller. Sale's prowess extended to county cup rugby, where they reigned undefeated from 1972 to 1987. The professional era saw challenges, but the club's fortunes changed with the arrival of Brian Kennedy, ensuring financial stability. Notable players like Jason Robinson from rugby league bolstered the squad. Sale clinched the 2002 European Challenge Cup, setting the stage for future successes.

    2005–06 Season: Championship Glory

    In the 2005–06 season, Sale Sharks, led by players like Bryan Redpath and Jason White, showcased exceptional prowess. They secured victory in 16 out of 22 games, clinching the Premiership title with a resounding 45–20 win against Leicester Tigers. This triumph underscored Sale's dominance and established them as a force to be reckoned with in English rugby.

    2006–2009: Challenges and Rebuilding

    Following their championship season, Sale faced adversity due to an injury crisis. The subsequent years presented challenges, but the club underwent a rebuilding phase. Philippe Saint-André's leadership, coupled with strategic player signings, set the foundation for future successes. Sale secured the 2005 European Challenge Cup and maintained a competitive edge in both domestic and European competitions.

    2012–2020: Steve Diamond's Impact

    The era under Steve Diamond, starting in 2012, marked a significant improvement for Sale. Despite a challenging 2012–13 season, they finished sixth in the Premiership and reached the quarter-finals of the European Challenge Cup. Sale's consistency in mid-table finishes from 2015 to 2020 showcased their stability in the competitive rugby landscape.

    2016–2020: Ownership Change and Mid-Table Stability

    The club's acquisition by an investment group in June 2016, led by Simon Orange, ushered in a new phase. Sale maintained a steady position in the mid-table during the 2016/17 to 2019/20 seasons. Notable signings like Faf de Klerk and Chris Ashton contributed to the team's competitive edge.

    2021–Present: Alex Sanderson's Leadership

    In January 2021, Alex Sanderson took the helm as Director of Rugby, returning to the club where he began his playing career. The 2021–22 season showcased promising results, finishing third in the Gallagher Premiership and reaching the quarter-finals of the Heineken Champions Cup. Despite narrowly missing out on the top four, Sale's performance highlighted their resurgence under Sanderson's leadership.

    2022/23: Stellar Signings and Playoff Heartbreak

    The 2022/23 season brought stellar signings like George Ford, Jonny Hill, and Tom O'Flaherty. Sale's outstanding form secured a second-place position, leading to a home playoff semi-final against Leicester. Although leading in the final against Saracens, two late tries resulted in a 35-25 defeat, showcasing the team's resilience amid playoff heartbreak.

    2023/24: Table-Topping Start

    The ongoing 2023/24 season sees Sale Sharks continuing their impressive form. With notable signings such as Luke Cowan-Dickie and Agustín Creevy, the team has emerged as early leaders, topping the table with five wins and one loss. This season promises to be another chapter in Sale's pursuit of excellence in English rugby.
