    • About The Highlanders

    The Highlanders, based in Dunedin, New Zealand, are a professional rugby union team known for their dynamic and entertaining style of play. Established in 1996 as part of the Super 12 competition (later expanded to Super 14 and Super Rugby), the Highlanders represent the Otago and Southland regions. The team has a rich history of passionate fan support and has produced memorable moments on the field. The Highlanders achieved Super Rugby success in 2015 when they secured their first championship by defeating the Hurricanes in a thrilling final. With a strong commitment to developing local talent and a reputation for tenacious performances, the Highlanders continue to be a force in the highly competitive world of southern hemisphere rugby.

    The Formative Years (1996-1997)

    The Highlanders, established in 1996 as part of the Super 12, had a promising start led by captain John Leslie and coach Gordon Hunter. The inaugural season saw victories, including a notable win against the Queensland Reds, but concluded with an eighth-place finish. In 1997, under coach Glenn Ross and captain Taine Randell, the team faced challenges, finishing last but showed resilience, defeating all four South African teams and reaching the semi-finals.

    Near Miss in 1999

    In 1999, under coach Tony Gilbert, the Highlanders rebounded, securing victories over formidable opponents and reaching the tournament final.     Laurie Mains Era (2002-2003)

    Under coach Laurie Mains in 2002, the Highlanders achieved notable victories, including a win over all four South African teams. The Gordon Hunter Memorial Trophy was introduced, and they finished fourth. In 2003, a coaching change occurred, with Greg Cooper taking over, and despite some successes, internal conflicts led to Mains' resignation.

    Rebuilding Phase (2004-2007)

    The subsequent years saw a rebuilding phase for the Highlanders, marked by a changing roster and coaching staff. In 2005, they achieved a six-game winning streak, but inconsistency followed in the ensuing seasons. After a player exodus post-2007 Rugby World Cup, the team faced challenges, resulting in their worst season in 2008.

    Jamie Joseph's Impact (2011-2015)

    In 2011, with Jamie Joseph as coach and Jamie Mackintosh as captain, the Highlanders witnessed a resurgence. They finished eighth after a strong start, showcasing improvement. The pinnacle came in 2015, winning the Super Rugby season by defeating the Hurricanes in a memorable final, marking a historic achievement for the franchise.

    Ups and Downs (2017-2021)

    The years from 2017 to 2021 were marked by highs and lows. Victories over the British & Irish Lions in 2017 and notable wins in 2018 showcased the team's potential. However, inconsistent performances and playoff struggles characterized these years, with varying results in Super Rugby Aotearoa and Super Rugby Trans-Tasman.

    Challenging Times (2022)

    The 2022 season proved challenging for the Highlanders. Despite a slow start, they showed resilience with crucial victories, earning a spot in the quarter-finals. However, a defeat to the Blues marked the end of a tough season, emphasizing the team's determination amid adversities
