    • About The Waratahs

    The Waratahs, based in Sydney, Australia, are a renowned rugby union team with a rich history in the sport. Established in 1882, they have been a dominant force in Australian rugby. The team competes in Super Rugby and has played a pivotal role in the development of elite rugby talent in the country. The Waratahs have produced numerous Wallabies, contributing significantly to the national team. Over the years, they have achieved success in the Super Rugby competition, clinching the championship in 2014. Known for their distinctive blue and sky blue jerseys, the Waratahs boast a strong fan base and continue to be a formidable presence in the world of Australian rugby.

    Amateur Glory Days: 1874–1963

    The New South Wales Rugby Union (NSWRU), established in 1874, laid the foundation for rugby competition in the region. By 1882, the first NSW team emerged victorious against Queensland, marking the beginning of a storied rugby tradition. The 1927/28 Waratahs' iconic tour introduced an unprecedented style of open, running rugby, leaving an indelible mark on the Australian game. The 1930s witnessed NSW's pinnacle with a triumphant victory against the South African Springboks in 1937. The 1963 establishment of the Sydney Rugby Union and the centennial celebration in 1974 underscored the enduring legacy of amateur rugby in NSW.

    Super 12 Triumphs and Challenges: 1996–2005

    The transition to professionalism brought both successes and hurdles for the Waratahs. In the inaugural Super 12 season (1996), they secured a mid-table position, with subsequent years witnessing a gradual climb. The breakthrough came in 2002 when the Waratahs, under coach Ewen McKenzie, reached the semifinals, showcasing their potential. The 2005 season marked their best, finishing second in the regular season but falling short in the final against the Crusaders. This era set the stage for the evolution of NSW rugby on the professional stage.

    Super 14 Rollercoaster: 2006–2010

    The Waratahs' journey in the expanded Super 14 brought moments of brilliance and disappointment. Despite finishing 3rd in 2006, a tumultuous season ensued, with the team facing challenges like Wendell Sailor's suspension. The following years saw fluctuating performances, including a disappointing 2007 season. However, 2008 showcased their resilience, securing the 500th win and reaching the Super 14 final in 2008, only to be defeated by the Crusaders. The last two Super14 seasons saw the Waratahs narrowly miss the finals and then progress to the semifinals in 2010.

    Super Rugby Evolution: 2011–Present

    The post-Super14 era brought a revamped competition, and the Waratahs faced ups and downs under coaches like Michael Foley and Michael Cheika. The years 2011 and 2012 saw the team struggling to reach the finals. However, a transformative period began in 2014 under Cheika's leadership. The Waratahs' exhilarating, attacking style led to a historic Super Rugby Championship win. The 2014 season stands as a testament to the team's resilience, marking a new era of success and exciting rugby for the New South Wales Waratahs.
