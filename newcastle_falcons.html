    History

    Newcastle Falcons, tracing their roots to the founding of Gosforth Football Club in 1877, have undergone several transformations. From Gosforth to Newcastle Gosforth in 1990 and eventually to Newcastle Falcons in 1997, each change marked a chapter in the club's journey.

    Professional Era

    The club's professional era began in 1996, with significant success under the leadership of Rob Andrew. In the 1997â€“98 season, they claimed the English Premiership title. Ownership shifts occurred, with Dave Thompson acquiring the club in 1999, leading to Powergen Cup victories in 2001 and 2004.

    Challenges and Rebuilding

    The mid-2000s saw notable player acquisitions, including Matt Burke, showcasing the club's ambition. However, challenges surfaced in the late 2000s, with managerial changes and relegation threats. Despite these, they secured promotion back to the Premiership in 2013 under Dean Richards.

    Recent Years

    Recent seasons have seen a mix of highs and lows, from the "Big One" success in 2018 to relegation in 2019. The club's resilience shone as they dominated the Championship in 2019â€“20, earning a return to the Premiership. The 2021â€“22 season brought attention to the club's commitment to addressing racism, highlighting challenges both on and off the field.

    Looking Ahead

    Newcastle Falcons, with a rich history of triumphs, challenges, and community engagement, continue to shape the narrative of English rugby. As they navigate the complexities of the modern game, the club remains a resilient force, symbolizing the enduring spirit of rugby in the North East.
