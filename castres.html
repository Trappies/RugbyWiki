    • About Castres

    Established in 1898, Castres Olympique, based in Castres, France, has etched its name in rugby history. The club, donning blue and white, has experienced notable successes, winning the French championship four times. Castres claimed their first title in the 1948-1949 season, setting the stage for a legacy of achievement. The subsequent championships in 1950 and 1993 further solidified Castres Olympique's reputation. In the 2012-2013 season, under the leadership of coach Christophe Urios, Castres clinched the Top 14 title, showcasing their enduring competitiveness. The club's journey reflects a commitment to excellence and a resilient spirit, making Castres Olympique a respected force in French rugby.

    Club Legacy

    Founded in 1898, Castres Olympique boasts a rich rugby history. The club, born from a gathering of municipal college alumni, swiftly rose through the ranks, claiming their first Coupe de France in 1948. Castres has since secured five French top-division championships, marking pivotal moments in the club's enduring legacy.

    Stade Pierre-Fabre

    Home to Castres Olympique, the Stade Pierre-Fabre, established in 1906, witnesses the team's triumphs. Despite its modest 12,500 capacity, this historic stadium has hosted memorable victories, including the 2012–13 French Rugby Union Championship where Castres emerged victorious against Toulon, etching another chapter in their storied journey.

    Blue and White Tradition

    Adorned in iconic blue and white kits, Castres Olympique embraces a distinctive color scheme dating back to its 1906 reformation. This symbolic choice reflects the club's commitment to rugby, breaking away from its multisport origins. The enduring blue and white tradition has become synonymous with Castres' resilience and sporting excellence.

    Triumphs and Controversies

    Castres Olympique's journey is not without its share of triumphs and controversies. From winning championships in 1993, 2013, and 2018 to overcoming challenges, including a disputed try in the 1993 final, the club has faced it all. These experiences have shaped Castres into a resilient force within French rugby, capturing the hearts of fans worldwide.

    Pierre Fabre's Legacy

    A pivotal moment in Castres' modern era was the takeover by Pierre Fabre in 1988. The pharmaceutical magnate revived the club's fortunes, leading to championship victories. His impact transcended rugby, with the renaming of the home stadium, previously Stade Pierre-Antoine, to Stade Pierre-Fabre in his honor. Fabre's legacy lives on, embedded in the heart of Castres Olympique.
