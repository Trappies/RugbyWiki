    • About The Hurricanes

    The Hurricanes, based in Wellington, New Zealand, stand as a formidable force in Southern Hemisphere rugby. Established in 1996 with the inception of Super Rugby, the team has a rich history of competitive performances. Known for their dynamic and expansive style of play, the Hurricanes have consistently been a title contender. Under the leadership of iconic players and skilled coaches, they have captured the hearts of rugby enthusiasts with their flair and commitment. The Hurricanes' ability to blend experienced players with emerging talents has been a key factor in their sustained success. Whether showcasing their skills at home in the Westpac Stadium or on the road, the Hurricanes continue to be a powerhouse in New Zealand and global rugby, leaving an indelible mark on the sport.

    Inaugural Challenges: 1996–1997

    The Hurricanes, originating in 1996 as part of the New Zealand Super 12 teams, faced a tough initiation under coach Frank Oliver and captain Bull Allen. Their debut against the Auckland Blues marked the first-ever Super 12 match, though they finished the season in ninth place. Despite a semifinal appearance in 1997, the team struggled to maintain consistent form during these early years.

    Expect the Unexpected: 1998–2002

    From 1998 to 2002, the Hurricanes, led by stars like Tana Umaga and Christian Cullen, were known for their unpredictable and attacking backline play. Despite their flair, they faced challenges in achieving consistent performances and struggled during crucial moments in matches. The team witnessed Jonah Lomu's return after the 1999 World Cup, adding strength to their lineup.

    New Era and Transformation: Since 2003

    In 2003, the arrival of coach Colin Cooper and captain Tana Umaga marked a turning point for the Hurricanes. They reached the semifinals for the second time in their history, propelled by the breakout performances of players like Ma'a Nonu. Despite challenges, including Christian Cullen's departure and Jonah Lomu's health issues, the team underwent a transformation, becoming a formidable force in Super Rugby.

    Super 14 Success and Financial Boost: 2006–2009

    The introduction of the Super 14 in 2006 saw the Hurricanes make their first Super Rugby final but narrowly lose to the Crusaders. Led by Rodney So'oialo, they experienced both on-pitch success and financial gain. Despite consistent semifinal appearances in 2008 and 2009, the Hurricanes couldn't secure the championship.

    2016 Triumph and Continued Contention: 2010–2021

    2016 proved historic for the Hurricanes as they secured their first Super Rugby title. Led by Dane Coles, they finished first overall, defeating the Lions in the final. The team continued to be contenders, reaching the quarterfinals in 2017 and narrowly missing the semifinals in 2018. The Hurricanes maintained their competitive edge, making playoffs consistently and displaying resilience in the face of challenges, including the disruptions caused by the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020.
