    • About The Lions

    The Lions rugby team, officially known as the Emirates Lions due to sponsorship, is a South African professional rugby union team based in Johannesburg. Established in 1996, the team competes in the United Rugby Championship and has a storied history in South African rugby. The Lions have consistently been a formidable force, reaching multiple finals in domestic competitions. They garnered international attention as one of the South African franchises participating in Super Rugby before the competition's restructuring. The team has produced numerous talented players who have gone on to represent the Springboks, contributing to South Africa's rugby success. The Emirates Lions continue to be a powerhouse in the South African rugby landscape, embodying the spirit and excellence of the sport in the region.

    Transvaal Triumph in Super 10 (1993–1995)

    Transvaal emerged victorious in the Super 10, a precursor to Super Rugby, clinching the title in 1993 by defeating Auckland 20–17 in the final. They continued their success by reaching the finals again in 1995, narrowly losing to Queensland, marking a significant era in Transvaal rugby history.

    Transvaal/Gauteng Lions' Super 12 Journey (1996–1997)

    As Super Rugby expanded with the Super 12 in 1996, Transvaal participated in the inaugural season, finishing 10th. Renamed Gauteng Lions in 1997, they had a more successful campaign, securing fifth place overall and narrowly missing out on a finals spot.

    Cats Roar into Super Rugby (1998–2005)

    The Cats, established in 1998, showcased their potential under coach Laurie Mains in 2000, reaching the semi-finals. Although their early success waned, the team continued to compete until the Super 12 expanded to the Super 14 in 2006, marking the end of the Cats era.

    Lions Roar into Super Rugby (2006–present)

    In 2006, the team transitioned to the Lions, adopting a new identity. Despite facing challenges, including a winless 2010 season, the Lions rebranded as the MTN Lions, introducing notable players. Relegated in 2012, they fought back through the Lions Challenge Series, earning promotion for the 2014 Super Rugby season.

    Resurgence and Super Rugby Success (2014–present)

    Determined to establish themselves as a top team, the Lions underwent significant changes, including coaching staff and playing style. Their resurgence saw them qualify for the finals in 2016, ultimately losing to the Hurricanes. The Lions continued their success in 2017, reaching the final again but falling short against the Crusaders.
