    • About Otago

    Established in 1881, the Otago rugby team stands as one of New Zealand's esteemed rugby entities with a rich history in the sport. Competing in the National Provincial Championship since its inception in 1976, Otago has been a formidable force. Their home games resonate within the iconic Forsyth Barr Stadium in Dunedin, a venue that has witnessed historic clashes. Affiliated with the Highlanders Super Rugby franchise, Otago wears blue and gold as their emblematic colors. Over the years, the team has showcased remarkable talent, contributing significantly to New Zealand's rugby legacy and producing players who have donned the prestigious All Blacks jersey. Otago's journey is a testament to the enduring spirit of New Zealand rugby.

    Otago Rugby Union

    Established in 1881, the Otago Rugby Football Union has been a stalwart in New Zealand rugby. Based in Dunedin and competing in the ITM Cup, the union has a proud history, celebrating its 125th year in 2006. With Forsyth Barr Stadium as its home ground, Otago has claimed the Ranfurly Shield seven times, notably in 2013, 2018, and 2020. The union's resilience was evident in 2012 when it faced liquidation but continued operations with the support of the Dunedin City Council. Otago boasts victories over formidable teams, including South Africa and the British and Irish Lions, showcasing its prowess on the international stage.

    Ranfurly Shield Glory

    Otago's legacy with the Ranfurly Shield is steeped in triumph and resilience. Holding the shield from 1935 to 1938, they defended it eight times and secured it again in 1938 for an additional five defenses. In a remarkable run between 1947 and 1950, Otago defended the shield 18 times before a narrow loss to Taranaki in 1950. The shield returned to Otago in 1957, defended once, but ultimately slipped away in a closely contested match against Taranaki, marked by memorable post-game banter between the coaches.

    Otago's Triumphs Against the Lions (1950, 1959, and 1966)

    Otago has etched its name in history with notable victories over the British and Irish Lions. In 1950 and 1959, Otago emerged victorious, showcasing their mettle against the touring Lions. The pinnacle came in 1966 at Carisbrook, where a capacity crowd witnessed Otago's 17–9 triumph. Wingers Dave Edwards and Maurie Collins left an indelible mark, scoring crucial tries to seal the memorable victory. The 1975 Otago rugby team further solidified their legacy, enjoying a stellar season with 15 wins and only two losses, including notable victories against Scotland and Auckland during an undefeated North Island tour.
