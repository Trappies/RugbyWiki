    • About The Moana Pasifka

    The Moana Pasifika rugby team stands as a symbol of unity, strength, and the rich rugby heritage of the Pacific Islands. Comprising players from Samoa, Tonga, and Fiji, the team represents a collective force in the world of rugby, embodying the passion and skill synonymous with Pacific rugby traditions. Established to provide a platform for Pacific Island talent and bolster representation in top-tier competitions, Moana Pasifika is not only a rugby team but a cultural ambassador. With a commitment to nurturing talent and fostering a sense of pride within the Pacific communities, Moana Pasifika adds a vibrant and dynamic dimension to the global rugby landscape, celebrating the diverse and impactful contributions of the Pacific Islands to the sport.

    Inception and Ambitions

    Moana Pasifika, born from the vision of uniting Pacific Island rugby talent, began as a one-off team in December 2020, initially challenging the Māori All Blacks. The goal was clear: secure a spot in the prestigious Super Rugby competition.

    Super Rugby Entry

    On April 14, 2021, Moana Pasifika received a conditional Super Rugby license, solidifying their aspiration. The unconditional license granted on July 12, 2021, confirmed their participation in the 2022 Super Rugby Pacific season, marking a historic entry into top-tier rugby.

    Historic Debut

    With their inaugural Super Rugby match held on March 4, 2022, Moana Pasifika embarked on a remarkable journey, showcasing their prowess and resilience in the fiercely competitive league.

    Strategic Base and Leadership

    Set to call South Auckland home until 2028, Moana Pasifika strategically positioned themselves in the heart of rugby culture. Under the leadership of coach Aaron Mauger and inaugural captain Sekope Kepu, the team aimed to make a lasting impact in the rugby arena.

    Triumphs and Milestones

    Despite an initial loss to the Crusaders, Moana Pasifika achieved a historic victory against the Hurricanes at Mt Smart Stadium, claiming their first Super Rugby win with a triumphant 24–19 score.
