    • About Stade Francais

    Stade Français, based in Paris, stands as one of France's most iconic rugby clubs. Founded in 1883, it boasts a rich history and a distinctive pink jersey that has become synonymous with flair and style. Over the years, Stade Français has been a powerhouse in French rugby, capturing numerous domestic titles, including the French Championship. The club's home matches are played at the Stade Jean-Bouin, creating an electric atmosphere. Stade Français has also left an indelible mark on European rugby, clinching the European Challenge Cup multiple times. Beyond its on-field successes, the club is celebrated for its commitment to entertaining rugby and vibrant fan culture, making Stade Français a captivating force in the world of rugby.

    The Birth of a Legacy (1883-1899)

    Founded in 1883 by a group of students in Paris, Stade Français embarked on its rugby journey, making history with its first French rugby union championship in 1892. The club quickly became a dominant force, securing multiple league titles in the late 19th century.

    Rise, Fall, and Resurgence (1900-2000)

    After a period of decline post-1908, entrepreneur Max Guazzini revitalized the club in 1992. Stade Français CASG emerged in 1995 through a merger, reclaiming its position in the top division. The team's resurgence culminated in a championship title in 1998, breaking a 90-year drought.

    Trials and Triumphs (2001-2011)

    Under coaches like John Connolly and Nick Mallet, Stade Français witnessed both domestic and European success. The team clinched the domestic league in 2003 and 2004 but faced heartbreak in the Heineken Cup finals. Financial challenges arose in 2010-11, threatening the club's status, but a strategic deal secured its future.

    Changing Guards (2011-2017)

    Thomas Savare took over from Max Guazzini in 2011, injecting funds to stabilize the club. However, a proposed merger with Racing 92 in 2017 faced strong player opposition, leading to its cancellation. The arrival of Swiss businessman Hans-Peter Wild in 2017 marked a new era for Stade Français.

    Rebuilding and Rebranding (2017 Onwards)

    The post-2017 era saw significant changes, with Heyneke Meyer leading a rebuilding phase. A renewed focus on player development and a revamped logo aimed to blend the club's rich history with a vision for the future. The (R)evolution, unveiled in 2018, symbolized a fresh start for Stade Français Paris.
