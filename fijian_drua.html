    • About The Fijian Drua

    The Fijian Drua rugby team, based in Fiji, is a powerhouse in the world of rugby, known for their electrifying and dynamic style of play. Founded in 2017, the Drua competes in various rugby competitions, including Super Rugby Pacific. The team serves as a breeding ground for Fijian talent, providing a platform for local players to showcase their skills on an international stage. With a strong emphasis on expansive and entertaining rugby, the Fijian Drua has garnered a reputation for thrilling performances that reflect the rich rugby culture of Fiji. As a vital component of Fiji's rugby landscape, the Drua not only contributes to the development of rugby talent but also adds a vibrant and competitive edge to the global rugby scene.

    Fijian Drua's National Rugby Championship Triumph (2018)

    In their second season in the National Rugby Championship (NRC) in 2018, the Fijian Drua exhibited dominance by finishing on top of the table after the regular season. They secured the Horan-Little Shield and went on to defeat Canberra Vikings in the semifinal before avenging their previous losses to Queensland Country in the final, clinching the 2018 NRC title with a thrilling 36–26 victory. This triumph showcased the Drua's prowess in Australian domestic rugby, solidifying their reputation as a force to be reckoned with.

    Fijian Drua's Super Rugby Entry (2022)

    After successfully competing in the NRC, the Fijian Drua elevated their status by gaining entry into the prestigious Super Rugby competition. Following the disbandment of the NRC and the expiration of licenses for other Super Rugby sides, the New Zealand Rugby Union granted the Drua a license to join the Super Rugby competition from the 2022 season onwards. Despite challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic, the Drua's inclusion marked a significant moment for Fijian rugby on the global stage. The team's participation in Super Rugby Pacific solidified their place among elite rugby franchises.

    Mick Byrne Leads Fijian Drua into Super Rugby (2022–2023)

    In preparation for their Super Rugby debut, the Fijian Drua appointed Mick Byrne as the head coach for the 2022 and 2023 seasons. This strategic move aimed to harness Byrne's expertise and guide the team through the challenges of the top-tier competition. The confirmation of their base in Suva, Fiji, for the 2022 season, and later in Australia due to travel restrictions, demonstrated the team's adaptability. With Lake Ainsworth Sports and Recreation Centre in Lennox Head, New South Wales as their base, the Fijian Drua embarked on their Super Rugby journey, representing Fijian rugby with pride and determination.

    Drua's Inaugural Super Rugby Pacific Campaign (2022)

    The anticipation for the Fijian Drua's inaugural Super Rugby Pacific campaign reached its peak with the announcement of the schedule for the 2022 season. The team's first match against the Blues in Auckland marked a historic moment in Fijian rugby. With Nemani Nagusa named captain for the 2022 season, the Drua aimed to make a lasting impact in Super Rugby, showcasing the Fijian spirit and style of play. The team's name, inspired by the traditional Fijian double canoe known as the drua, symbolized their commitment to speed, agility, and flair on the rugby field. The sky blue, black, and white colors represented the team's identity throughout their journey in both the NRC and Super Rugby.
