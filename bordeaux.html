    • About Bordeaux

    The Bordeaux Beagles rugby team is a formidable presence in the French rugby scene, renowned for their skill, determination, and passionate fanbase. Based in the vibrant city of Bordeaux, the team competes in top-tier competitions, showcasing their prowess on the field. Known for their distinctive blend of attacking flair and solid defense, the Beagles have left an indelible mark in French rugby history. The team's colors, a nod to Bordeaux's rich wine heritage, adorn the players as they navigate the challenges of domestic and international competitions. With a commitment to excellence and a roster of talented players, the Bordeaux Beagles continue to be a force to be reckoned with, contributing to the dynamic and competitive landscape of French rugby.

    Bordeaux Beagles' Rise to the Top

    Union Bordeaux Bègles (UBB) emerged as a powerhouse in French rugby after winning the promotion playoffs in the 2010–11 season, securing their place in the prestigious Top 14 league. Founded in 2006 through the merger of Stade Bordelais and Club Athlétique Bordeaux-Bègles Gironde, UBB has donned claret and white while playing at the iconic Stade Chaban-Delmas. The amalgamation aimed to unify Bordeaux's rugby landscape, which had long been divided between the Stade Bordelais and CA Bordeaux-Bègles-Gironde. The journey wasn't without challenges, as both clubs had experienced highs and lows before their merger.

    Bordeaux's Rugby Renaissance

    For years, Bordeaux grappled with the absence of a dominant rugby club, with the Stade Bordelais and CA Bordeaux-Bègles-Gironde competing for local supremacy. While Stade Bordelais enjoyed national success in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, CA Béglais had its shining moments in 1969 and 1991. However, the dawn of the new millennium brought hardship, relegation, and financial struggles. The plan to merge both clubs in 2005 faced staunch opposition from supporters, but influential figures and local businesses advocated for unity. Bordeaux Rugby Metropolis was established in 2006, leading to the creation of Union Bordeaux Bègles and a new era in Bordeaux's rugby history.

    Bordeaux's Meteoric Ascent

    Following the merger, Bordeaux Rugby Metropolis facilitated the union, forming a Professional Sporting Public Limit Company (SASP) that saw the amalgamation of professional squads. Although the historic clubs retained their youth teams, the combined effort propelled the new team into Pro D2, inheriting the place of Stade Bordelais. With a budget of €3.6 million initially, Laurent Marti took the helm as president, overseeing the club's growth. Their ambition to reclaim elite status materialized in the 2010–11 season when they clinched victory in the finals against SC Albi, earning promotion to the Top 14. The team's ascent continued, with Gavekal, a Hong Kong investment company, acquiring a 10% stake in 2015, solidifying their position in French rugby.
