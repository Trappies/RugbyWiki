    • About Connacht

    Connacht Rugby, based in Galway, Ireland, has forged a spirited path in the world of rugby. Established in 1885, the team competes in various leagues, with a notable presence in the United Rugby Championship and European competitions. Despite being one of the smaller provinces in Ireland, Connacht has built a reputation for resilience and a never-say-die attitude. The historic highlight came in 2016 when they clinched the Pro12 title, marking a momentous achievement for the team and its passionate fanbase. With a commitment to nurturing local talent and embracing an expansive style of play, Connacht Rugby continues to captivate audiences and contribute to the vibrant rugby culture in Ireland and beyond.

    Foundation and Amateur Era (1885–1995)

    Connacht Rugby traces its roots back to its establishment on December 8, 1885, with the formation of the Connacht Branch of the Irish Rugby Football Union. Founded by six teams, including Ballinasloe and Castlebar, the province now boasts 25 senior clubs. Over the years, Connacht has produced notable players like Henry Anderson and Aengus McMorrow, who paved the way for Connacht representation in Ireland and beyond.

    Early Professional Years (1995–2003)

    With rugby union declared an "open" game in 1995, the professional era began. Connacht participated in the inaugural Heineken Cup in the 1995–96 season and experienced success in the 1997–98 Challenge Cup, defeating English and French teams. Despite financial challenges and threats to its professional status in 2003, Connacht persevered, thanks in part to a public protest and player support.

    Michael Bradley Era (2003–2010)

    Michael Bradley took the helm in 2003, navigating Connacht through financial constraints and achieving notable success in the European Challenge Cup. Despite struggles in the Celtic League, Connacht's resilience shone in cup competitions. Bradley's tenure culminated in the 2009–10 season, marked by a historic Challenge Cup run and increased fan support.

    Eric Elwood as Head Coach (2010–2013)

    Following Bradley, former Connacht and Ireland fly-half Eric Elwood took charge. The team's entry into the Heineken Cup in 2011–12 heightened its popularity. Elwood's tenure saw improved performances, notably reaching the Pro12 semi-finals in 2015, and the team's first major trophy, winning the Pro12 in 2016.

    Pat Lam Takes Over (2013–2017)

    Pat Lam, a former Samoa international, succeeded Elwood. Under Lam's leadership, Connacht achieved historic success, winning the Pro12 in 2016. Despite challenges, including injuries and European restructuring, Connacht's popularity soared, and average attendance reached new heights.

    Post-Lam Era (2017–Present)

    Kieran Keane and later Andy Friend took charge post-Lam. Connacht continued its presence in European competitions, consistently qualifying for the Challenge Cup knockout stages. Friend's impact was evident in improved league performances, securing a third-place finish in the 2018–19 Pro14. The team reached the knockout stages of the 2021-22 Heineken Champions Cup, marking another milestone in Connacht's storied history. Friend's subsequent promotion to Director of Rugby reflects a positive trajectory for Connacht Rugby.
