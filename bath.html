    • About Bath

    The Bordeaux Beagles rugby team is a formidable presence in the French rugby scene, renowned for their skill, determination, and passionate fanbase. Based in the vibrant city of Bordeaux, the team competes in top-tier competitions, showcasing their prowess on the field. Known for their distinctive blend of attacking flair and solid defense, the Beagles have left an indelible mark in French rugby history. The team's colors, a nod to Bordeaux's rich wine heritage, adorn the players as they navigate the challenges of domestic and international competitions. With a commitment to excellence and a roster of talented players, the Bordeaux Beagles continue to be a force to be reckoned with, contributing to the dynamic and competitive landscape of French rugby.

    The Pioneer Years (1865–1900)

    Founded in 1865 by members of Lansdown Cricket Club, Bath Football Club emerged as a winter pastime. The club's nomadic existence during the 1800s laid the groundwork for its resilient spirit. Playing against local and, eventually, Welsh clubs, Bath faced defeats but set the stage for its future in rugby.

    Jack Rowell's Revolution (1978–1995)

    Jack Rowell's tenure from 1978 heralded a transformative era for Bath Rugby. Rowell shifted the club's ethos, bringing in professionalism and assembling a powerful side. The '80s and early '90s saw Bath dominate the John Player and Pilkington Cups, establishing themselves as a formidable force in English rugby.

    Grand Slam Glory (1993–1994)

    In the 1993–94 season, Bath achieved a unique "Grand Slam" of titles. Winning the league, Pilkington Cup, Middlesex Sevens, and Worthington Tens, Bath showcased unparalleled prowess. This period marked the pinnacle of Bath's achievements during the twilight years of amateurism in English rugby.

    Heineken Cup Triumph (1997–1998)

    Despite struggles post-professionalism, Bath Rugby made history in 1998 by becoming the first British club to lift the Heineken Cup. Captained by Andy Nicol, Bath secured a thrilling 19–18 victory over French club Brive in the final held in Bordeaux. Jon Callard's scoring heroics sealed Bath's place in rugby lore.

    European Challenge Cup Success (2007–2008)

    In 2008, Bath ended a decade-long trophy drought by winning the European Challenge Cup. Facing Worcester Warriors in the final, Bath, led by outgoing skipper Steve Borthwick, triumphed 24–16. The win signaled a resurgence for Bath, showcasing their ability to compete at the highest level in European competitions.

    A New Chapter (2010 Onwards)

    The 2010 ownership change brought new plans for Bath Rugby. With ambitions to create a new headquarters at Farleigh House and build a new stadium, Bath embarked on a journey to revitalize the club. Coaching changes, including the return of Brian Ashton, set the stage for a new era, and the club sought to recapture past glories.

    Recent Challenges and Renewed Hope (2021–2022)

    Facing challenges in the 2021–22 season, Bath Rugby underwent a significant restructuring. The appointment of Johann van Graan as Head of Rugby marked a strategic move to stabilize and rebuild the club's fortunes. Van Graan, joining from Munster, brought a wealth of experience and a long-term vision for Bath Rugby's success.
