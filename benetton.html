    • About Benetton

    Benetton Rugby, based in Treviso, Italy, is a club that transcends the traditional rugby landscape. Founded in 1932, the team initially focused on representing the Veneto region. However, the club underwent a transformation, both in its name and its approach, becoming Benetton Rugby in 1979. What sets Benetton apart is its commitment to diversity, reflected not only in its players' nationalities but also in its inclusive philosophy. The team has become a melting pot of talent, featuring players from various backgrounds, contributing to a unique blend of styles on the rugby pitch. Benetton Rugby's journey exemplifies the power of sport to bring people together, fostering unity in a sport that thrives on camaraderie and shared passion.

    Italian Powerhouse Since 1932

    Benetton Rugby, formerly known as Benetton Treviso, stands as an Italian rugby icon based in Treviso, Veneto. Founded in 1932, the club boasts an impressive history, winning 15 Italian national championships. Owned by the renowned Benetton clothing company since 1979, the team competes in the United Rugby Championship, European Rugby Challenge Cup, and European Champions Cup. Notable players like Alessandro Zanni and Leonardo Ghiraldini have emerged from Treviso, contributing to the Italian national team. The club's commitment to excellence extends to its women's team, the Red Panthers, clinching 16 national championships since 1982.

    Amateur Triumphs and Professional Dominance

    In the amateur era (1932–1995), Treviso secured multiple Italian premierships and cups. The transition to professionalism in 1995 marked a golden era, dominating the Italian league and clinching the championship ten times between 1997 and 2010. Benetton Treviso's European presence began in the inaugural 1995–96 Heineken Cup, with consistent participation since. The club's move to the Pro12 in 2010 elevated its competition level, facing teams from Ireland, Wales, and Scotland. Despite challenges in subsequent seasons, a strategic coaching shift in 2016/2017, led by Kieran Crowley, propelled Treviso to a commendable 10th place.

    Evolution into Benetton Rugby

    The 2017/2018 season brought a significant change, with the Pro14 introducing two South African teams and dividing into conferences. Benetton Rugby, as it was renamed, embraced this shift and showcased notable improvement, narrowly missing the European Rugby Champions Cup play-offs in 2017/2018. The club's commitment to the future is evident in the establishment of an Academy Under 23 and a territorial Academy Under 19 from the 2022/23 season, ensuring a steady influx of talent and a promising trajectory for Benetton Rugby.
