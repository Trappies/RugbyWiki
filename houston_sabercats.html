    • About The Houston Sabercats

    The Houston SaberCats, a formidable force in Major League Rugby (MLR), bring the spirit of rugby to Texas with their dynamic presence. Established as an expansion team in the 2018 MLR season, the SaberCats quickly established themselves as contenders. Playing their home matches at the Aveva Stadium in Houston, the team has garnered a loyal fan base. Owned by a group of investors led by Marty Power, the SaberCats have consistently showcased their commitment to the growth of rugby in the United States. With a blend of local talent and international experience, the SaberCats continue to contribute to the thriving rugby culture in Houston and the broader MLR community.

    Houston SaberCats: A Rugby Powerhouse in Texas

    The Houston SaberCats, founded in 2017, are a dominant force in American professional rugby, competing in Major League Rugby (MLR). Originating from a founding amateur rugby club, the SaberCats were pioneers in transitioning to professional status and were the first MLR team to boast a dedicated rugby stadium. Their home field, SaberCats Stadium, opened in April 2019, symbolizing their commitment to the sport's growth. The city of Houston played a pivotal role, contributing $3.2 million to the stadium's construction. The venue, initially named "Aveva Stadium" through a naming rights deal, stands as a testament to the SaberCats' dedication to fostering a rugby culture in Texas.

    SaberCats Stadium: A Rugby Haven

    Houston SaberCats proudly call SaberCats Stadium their home, where the spirit of rugby comes alive. This state-of-the-art venue, initially named "Aveva Stadium," emerged from a collaborative effort with the City of Houston. Opened in April 2019, the stadium reflects the SaberCats' vision of providing a top-tier facility for MLR competitions. British multinational software and technology company Aveva secured a three-year naming rights deal, giving the stadium its initial name. The venue's significance extends beyond rugby, serving as the permanent training ground for Major League Soccer's Houston Dynamo and the Houston Dash women's soccer team.

    Home Turf Journey: Dyer Stadium to SaberCats Stadium

    During the 2018 regular season, the SaberCats played home matches at Dyer Stadium and trained at Athlete Training and Health in Houston. Constellation Field, home of the Sugar Land Skeeters, hosted part of the 2019 season and exhibition matches in 2017 and 2018. A pivotal moment came in April 2019 when, as the new pitch at SaberCats Stadium healed, the team returned to Dyer Stadium for one match against San Diego before reoccupying their cherished SaberCats Stadium. This journey underlines the SaberCats' resilience and commitment to providing fans with a thrilling rugby experience.
