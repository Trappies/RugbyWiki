    • About Pumas

    The Pumas, hailing from the province of Mpumalanga, are a formidable presence in South African rugby. Established as a professional rugby union team, the Pumas bring a unique flair to the country's rugby landscape. Their home base at the Mbombela Stadium in Nelspruit serves as a fortress where fans gather to witness thrilling rugby battles. Clad in their distinctive pink and grey jerseys, the Pumas showcase the pride of Mpumalanga on the rugby field. Competing in top-tier competitions like the Currie Cup and the United Rugby Championship, the South African Pumas embody the spirit of the game, blending skill and determination. As they continue to make their mark in domestic and international rugby, the Pumas from Mpumalanga are a force to be reckoned with, symbolizing the rich rugby heritage of South Africa.

    Airlink Pumas: Roaring Triumphs at Mbombela Stadium

    Competing in the Premier Division of the Currie Cup and the Northern Section of the Vodacom Cup, the Airlink Pumas are a force to be reckoned with in South African rugby. Hailing from Mpumalanga Province, formerly known as the South Eastern Transvaal, the team has a rich history since its formation in 1969. The Mbombela Stadium in Mbombela serves as their battleground, echoing with the cheers of passionate fans from Witbank, Middelburg, Ermelo, and beyond. In a historic moment, the Pumas clinched their first Currie Cup title in 2022, marking a triumphant chapter in their journey. Led by seasoned coach Jimmy Stonehouse, the Pumas continue to defy expectations, exemplifying resilience and skill on the rugby field.

    Pumas' Roots: South Eastern Transvaal Legacy

    Formed in 1969 as South Eastern Transvaal, the Pumas carry the legacy of one of the younger rugby unions in South Africa. Despite facing challenges in their early years, the team's determination shone through. In 2005 and 2009, the Pumas claimed victory in the Currie Cup First Division, earning well-deserved promotions to the Premier Division. The journey to success has been a testament to the Pumas' commitment to excellence and the support of their loyal fan base across Mpumalanga Province.

    Coaching Evolution: From Struggles to Blue Bulls Upset

    The coaching evolution of the Pumas reflects their journey from struggles to remarkable upsets. In 2006, under the joint coaching of Chris Grobler and Danie Gerber, the team faced a challenging season, failing to secure a win. However, a turning point came in 2010 when seasoned coach Jimmy Stonehouse took the helm. The Mbombela Stadium witnessed a historic moment as the Pumas triumphed over the Blue Bulls with a narrow 22–21 victory. This victory marked a shift in fortunes, showcasing the Pumas' resilience and determination to overcome the odds in the Currie Cup competition.
