    • About Edinburgh

    Edinburgh Rugby stands as a cornerstone of Scottish rugby, competing in both domestic and international arenas. Established in 1996 as part of the professionalization of the sport, the team has evolved to become a powerhouse in the Guinness Pro14 and European competitions. With their home at Murrayfield Stadium, Edinburgh's blue and black colors symbolize the city's rich history and its passionate rugby community. Over the years, the club has seen remarkable players don its jersey, contributing to memorable moments on the pitch. The commitment to developing local talent, combined with strategic international signings, showcases Edinburgh Rugby's dedication to a balanced and competitive squad. As they continue to write chapters in Scottish rugby history, Edinburgh Rugby remains a force to be reckoned with, embodying the pride and fervor of the nation's rugby legacy.

    District Origins (1872)

    Founded in 1872, Edinburgh District played in the world's first-ever inter-district match against Glasgow District. The club's roots delve into the rich history of Scottish rugby, laying the foundation for what would later become Edinburgh Rugby.

    Professional Era Dawns (1996)

    With the advent of professional rugby in 1995, the Scottish Rugby Union (SRU) recognized the need for a more competitive structure. In 1996, Edinburgh District transitioned into Edinburgh Rugby to partake in the Heineken Cup, marking a significant step in the evolution of Scottish rugby.

    Mergers and Rebranding (19992006)

    Navigating financial challenges, Edinburgh Rugby experienced mergers and rebranding. The club joined forces with Border Reivers and underwent several name changes, adapting to the evolving landscape of professional rugby in Scotland.

    Private Ownership (200607)

    In a groundbreaking move in 2006, Edinburgh became Scotland's first private franchise, backed by businessmen Alex and Bob Carruthers. This shift aimed to secure the team's future amid financial uncertainties, leading to both successes and challenges on and off the pitch.

    Return to SRU Control (2007)

    A funding dispute in 2007 led to Edinburgh's temporary withdrawal from the SRU. The subsequent resolution saw the termination of the franchise agreement, returning Edinburgh Rugby to the direct control of the SRU.

    Andy Robinson's Era (20072009)

    Under coach Andy Robinson, Edinburgh Rugby showcased promising performances, notably reaching the Heineken Cup semi-final in the 200809 season. Robinson's departure in 2009 marked a transition period for the club.

    Michael Bradley's Reign (20112013)

    Michael Bradley took the helm in 2011, overseeing a blend of experienced players and emerging talents. Despite challenges, Edinburgh's 201112 Heineken Cup campaign stands out as a historic achievement, reaching the semi-finals.

    Alan Solomons' Era (20132016)

    Alan Solomons, appointed in 2013, focused on rebuilding the team. While achieving an eighth-place finish in his first season, subsequent years showed improvement, with notable successes in the European Challenge Cup.

    Richard Cockerill's Impact (20172021)

    Richard Cockerill's tenure brought Edinburgh back to the Pro14 playoffs in the 201718 season. His emphasis on player development saw numerous Edinburgh players make international debuts, and some even joined the British and Irish Lions.

    Mike Blair's Leadership (20212023)

    Following Cockerill's departure, Mike Blair took charge in 2021, bringing fresh perspectives and new signings. Blair's coaching journey, announced to conclude in 2023, signals another transition for Edinburgh Rugby.

    Sean Everitt's Arrival (2023)

    In July 2023, Sean Everitt was recruited as "Senior Coach," poised to lead Edinburgh Rugby into the 2023/24 season. His appointment marks the next chapter in the ongoing saga of a club deeply embedded in the fabric of Scottish rugby.
