    Miami Sharks: Ready to Make Waves in MLR

    The Miami Sharks, set to debut in the 2024 Major League Rugby (MLR) season, are poised to become a formidable force in the league. Founded in 2023, the team is owned by a trio of influential Argentine businessmen, including Ronaldo "Kony" Strazzolini, Alejandro Macfarlane, and Mercado Libre chairman Marcos Galperin. The leadership team is equally impressive, with former Toronto Arrows executive Mark Winokur serving as CEO, Mariano Marco as COO, and José Pellicena, an experienced rugby figure, as the head coach. The Sharks have already made headlines with the signing of Argentine international scrum-half Tomás Cubelli, showcasing their intent to assemble a competitive roster.

    Dynamic Colors and Identity: A Miami Vice Tribute

    The Sharks' identity is a vibrant tribute to Miami's iconic past, drawing inspiration from the 1980s TV series "Miami Vice." The crest features a blue and white shark with pink accents, embodying the team's flair and Miami's cultural resonance. The main colors, neon-styled pink and blue, pay homage to the aesthetics of "Miami Vice" and the popular Miami Heat "Vice" uniforms. With a nod to the show's pastel color palette, the Sharks have unveiled three distinctive playing kits, each capturing the essence of the vibrant city.

    Strategic Partnerships and Home Turf

    In a strategic move, the Sharks announced a partnership with Inter Miami CF, securing the AutoNation Sports Field as their home venue with a capacity for up to 5,000 spectators. This collaboration enhances the team's presence in the South Florida sports landscape. The alignment with Inter Miami CF demonstrates the Sharks' commitment to creating a dynamic rugby experience while expanding their fanbase.

    Argentine Roots and Global Vision

    The Sharks proudly embrace their Argentine roots, with two of the three owners having played rugby union in Argentina for prestigious clubs like Belgrano Athletic Club and Club San Andrés. The ownership, including prominent figures like Marcos Galperin, reflects a deep connection to Latin America. As the wealthiest man in Argentina, Galperin's involvement underscores the ambition to ignite Latin passion in South Florida. The coaching staff and executives, with ties to Argentina, showcase the team's commitment to blending a rich rugby heritage with a global vision.

    Ownership Powerhouse: Argentine Business Titans

    Led by financial services firm Magna Capital president Ronaldo "Kony" Strazzolini, natural gas distribution company Camuzzi Gas main shareholder Alejandro Macfarlane, and e-commerce giant Mercado Libre founder and CEO Marcos Galperin, the Sharks boast an ownership powerhouse. With an estimated net worth of $5 billion, Galperin leads a group of investors, including Santiago Ocampo, Martín Migoya, Edgardo Defortuna, and Gabriel Martino, poised to propel the Miami Sharks to success in Major League Rugby.
