    • About La Rochelle

    Founded in 1898, Stade Rochelais, commonly known as La Rochelle, embodies the maritime spirit of its coastal hometown on the Bay of Biscay. Despite historical ebbs, La Rochelle surged to prominence in the professional era, culminating in their Top 14 title triumph in the 2020-2021 season. The Stade Marcel-Deflandre, resonating with passionate support, serves as the harbor where the club's ambitions and the city's maritime heritage converge, showcasing a resilient journey that mirrors the enduring spirit of La Rochelle.

    La Rochelle Rugby Club - Founded in 1898

    Established in 1898, La Rochelle Rugby Club, commonly known as Stade Rochelais, stands as a prominent professional rugby union team in France. Adorned in distinctive yellow and black colors, the club's first-team competes in the highly competitive Top 14 league, showcasing a legacy rooted in tradition.

    European Rugby Champions Cup Dominance

    La Rochelle has etched its name in rugby history by winning the prestigious European Rugby Champions Cup twice, triumphing over Leinster in both the 2022 and 2023 finals. Despite not securing domestic championship victories against Toulouse, the club's European success highlights its prowess on the international stage.

    Stade Marcel-Deflandre - Home of La Rochelle

    Playing its home matches at Stade Marcel-Deflandre, La Rochelle takes pride in its iconic stadium. The club has garnered immense support, with fervent fans contributing to consistently sold-out home matches. Owned by several shareholders, including club president Vincent Merling, La Rochelle is renowned for the passionate dedication of its supporters.
