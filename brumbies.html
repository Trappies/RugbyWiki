    • About The Brumbies

    The Brumbies, based in Canberra, Australia, stand as one of the premier rugby teams in the Southern Hemisphere. Established in 1996 to compete in the Super 12 (now Super Rugby), the Brumbies quickly asserted themselves as a dominant force. With a commitment to expansive and skillful play, they secured their maiden Super Rugby title in 2001. Known for their strategic game plans and a strong emphasis on player development, the Brumbies have consistently been contenders in the competition. Over the years, they have produced numerous Wallabies, contributing significantly to Australian rugby. The team's legacy is marked not only by their on-field successes but also by their role in shaping the rugby landscape in the region. The Brumbies remain a symbol of excellence and resilience in the world of professional rugby.

    Early Years of ACT Rugby

    Rugby's roots in the ACT date back to 1899, and the ACT Rugby Union (ACTRU) was officially established in 1937. The First Grade competition began in 1938 with four clubs, laying the foundation for the region's rugby legacy. Notable moments include a representative ACT side facing the All Blacks in 1938 and the ACT's first international victory in 1973 against Tonga. The adoption of the name 'Kookaburras' in 1989 marked a significant step, and the Kookaburras' impressive win against the Waratahs in 1994 paved the way for the ACT's professional era.

    Birth of the Brumbies (1995–1996)

    Formally established on December 9, 1995, in Jindabyne, the ACT Brumbies emerged as Australia's third provincial team with the inception of Super Rugby. Under coach Rod Macqueen, the inaugural Super 12 season in 1996 saw the Brumbies finish fifth. Despite a loss in the 1997 final, the foundation was laid for future successes, setting the stage for the Brumbies' role as a dominant force in Australian rugby.

    Super 12 Era Triumphs (1998–2005)

    Under the leadership of Eddie Jones and later David Nucifora, the Brumbies experienced highs and lows. They reached the Super 12 Final in 2000 and 2001, winning the latter and becoming the first team outside of New Zealand to claim the title. Laurie Fisher took over in 2004, guiding the Brumbies to another Final victory, solidifying their place in Super Rugby history.

    Transition to Super 14 (2006–2010)

    The Brumbies faced new challenges in the expanded Super 14 era, narrowly missing the playoffs in 2006. Despite the shift, they showcased their prowess by winning the Australian Provincial Championship. The team consistently maintained a competitive edge, demonstrating their resilience and commitment to excellence.

    Super Rugby Evolution (2011–present)

    With a changing coaching lineup, the Brumbies continued their legacy in Super Rugby. Jake White's tenure saw strong performances, including defeating the British & Irish Lions in 2013. The team reached the finals in 2013, securing victories but falling short in the championship final against the Waikato Chiefs. The Brumbies remain a formidable force, contributing to the rich tapestry of Australian rugby.
