    • About Wellington

    Wellington, a rugby powerhouse in New Zealand, boasts a rich rugby history and a team that competes at the highest levels. The Wellington rugby team, commonly known as the Wellington Lions, represents the Wellington Rugby Football Union (WRFU) in various domestic competitions. Their home matches are played at the iconic Sky Stadium in Wellington, creating an electric atmosphere for fans. The team's legacy includes numerous successes in New Zealand's provincial competitions, showcasing the region's commitment to nurturing top-tier rugby talent. As a key contributor to the country's rugby heritage, the Wellington Lions continue to uphold a tradition of excellence on the field.

    Wellington Rugby Football Union (Wellington Lions)

    Wellington Rugby Football Union (known as Wellington Lions) is the governing body for rugby union in the Wellington Region of New Zealand. The professional rugby union team, Wellington Lions, competes in the Mitre 10 Cup, challenging for the prestigious Ranfurly Shield. The team's primary stadium is Sky Stadium in Wellington.

    Super Rugby and National Representative Rugby

    Wellington contributes significantly to the Super Rugby team, Hurricanes, forming the core of the squad. The Wellington Lions participate in the Mitre 10 Cup, winning titles in 1978, 1981, 1986, 2000, and 2022. They secured the Championship in 2017 and exhibit their prowess at Sky Stadium. Despite notable players, the team faced inconsistencies.

    Ranfurly Shield Legacy

    Wellington has a storied history with the Ranfurly Shield, having won and defended it multiple times. Their early successes date back to 1904, and they recently held the shield after a victory against Auckland in 2008, maintaining it for four defenses.

    Bunnings NPC Representation

    The Wellington Lions represent the Wellington Rugby Football Union in the Bunnings NPC, showcasing their prowess in New Zealand's top-tier provincial competition.
