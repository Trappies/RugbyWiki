    • About Oyonnax

    Oyonnax Rugby, based in Oyonnax, France, is a professional rugby union club competing in the French league system. Established in 1909, the club has experienced periods of both success and challenges. Oyonnax reached prominence in the early 2010s, earning promotions to the Top 14, the top tier of French rugby. Their tenacious playstyle and dedicated fan base contributed to their rise through the ranks. While they faced relegation battles, Oyonnax consistently displayed resilience, showcasing the spirit of a club deeply rooted in its community. The Stade Charles-Mathon serves as the team's home ground, witnessing the highs and lows of Oyonnax Rugby's ongoing journey in the dynamic landscape of French rugby.

    Oyonnax Rugby in Top 14

    Oyonnax Rugby, is a prominent French professional rugby union club competing in the esteemed Top 14 league. The club, founded in 1949 and situated in the Ain mountainous department, proudly dons black and red jerseys during their fiercely contested matches at the Stade Charles-Mathon, boasting an elevation of 532 meters (1,745 feet), making it the highest stadium in the league with a capacity of 11,400.

    Historical Evolution

    Originally established in 1909 as Club Sportif Oyonnaxien, the club underwent a name change to Union Sportive Oyonaxienne in 1940 due to the enforced unification of sports clubs by the Vichy régime. Oyonnax navigated through regional leagues until their ascent to the First Division in 1967, only to face relegation shortly after. The team's resilience led to a return to Group B of the First Division (essentially the Second Division) in 1988. A pivotal moment occurred in 2003 when Oyonnax reached the Fédérale 1 final, eventually transitioning to the professional Pro D2. Their triumphant 2012/13 season saw them clinch the Pro D2 title, securing promotion to the prestigious Top 14. Despite a brief relegation in the 2016/17 season, Oyonnax swiftly reclaimed their spot in the Top 14.
