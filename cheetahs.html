    • About Cheetahs

    The Free State Cheetahs, based in Bloemfontein, South Africa, stand as a rugby powerhouse with a rich legacy. Established in 1895, the franchise has been a dominant force in South African rugby. The Cheetahs have a storied history in domestic competitions, notably excelling in the Currie Cup. Their commitment to nurturing talent is evident through the development of world-class players, contributing significantly to the Springboks, South Africa's national team. The team's vibrant orange jersey, reflecting the colors of the Free State province, symbolizes their fierce and tenacious style of play. The Free State Cheetahs continue to be a formidable presence in the rugby landscape, embodying the spirit and passion of South African rugby.

    Legacy and Transformation

    The Toyota Cheetahs, based in Bloemfontein, South Africa, boast a dynamic history transitioning from Super Rugby to the Pro14 (now United Rugby Championship) and currently competing in the EPCR Challenge Cup since 2022. Established as one of the new franchises in the expanded Super 14 competition in 2006, the Cheetahs quickly made their mark, demonstrating resilience and skill. The franchise's vibrant orange jersey reflects the colors of the Free State province, and their commitment to excellence extends to their successful foray into European competitions.

    Central Cheetahs' Striking Strips

    The Toyota Cheetahs, recognized for sponsorship as such, sport distinctive orange jerseys with white collars and trim, paying homage to the Free State Currie Cup side. The primary strip exudes energy with black shorts and orange socks, while the alternative jersey offers a stylish twist in white with an orange collar and trim. These striking strips encapsulate the team's identity, reflecting a potent blend of tradition and modernity.

    Historic Super Rugby Journey

    Entering Super Rugby in 2006, the Cheetahs emerged as a force, displaying prowess on both local and international fronts. Their inaugural season showcased a remarkable tenth-place finish, and over the years, they continued to evolve. Victories against formidable opponents, like the New South Wales Waratahs in 2011, demonstrated their ability to compete at the highest level. The franchise's journey reflects resilience, growth, and a commitment to exciting, high-quality rugby.
