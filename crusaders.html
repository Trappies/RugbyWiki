    • About The Crusaders

    Established in 1996, the Crusaders are a powerhouse in New Zealand rugby, based in Christchurch. This formidable team has consistently showcased excellence, winning numerous Super Rugby titles. With a strong emphasis on teamwork, strategic play, and a rich rugby culture, the Crusaders have become synonymous with success in the Southern Hemisphere. Their roster boasts top-tier talent, and under the guidance of skilled coaches, the Crusaders have not only dominated the New Zealand conference but also clinched titles, reinforcing their status as one of the most successful franchises in Super Rugby history. Known for their passionate fanbase and relentless pursuit of victory, the Crusaders continue to set the standard for excellence in the world of rugby.

    Crusaders' Inaugural Campaign: 1996–1997

    The Crusaders, established in 1996 as part of the Super 12, initially faced challenges, finishing at the bottom in their inaugural season. Led by captain Richard Loe and coach Vance Stewart, the team struggled, securing only two wins. The following year, under new leadership with Todd Blackadder as captain and Wayne Smith as coach, the Crusaders showed improvement, winning five matches and finishing in sixth place.

    Hat Trick Triumph: 1998–2000

    The Crusaders secured their first Super 12 title in 1998, overcoming a slow start to the season. Their remarkable turnaround included a seven-game winning streak, culminating in a thrilling 36–32 semi-final victory over the Coastal Sharks. In the final against the favored Blues, the Crusaders emerged victorious with a dramatic 20–13 win. The success continued in 1999, as they claimed their second consecutive title, defeating the Highlanders 24–19 in the final.

    Developing a Legacy: 2001–2005

    After a brief dip in 2001, the Crusaders roared back in 2002, completing an undefeated season and clinching their fourth title. Led by captain Reuben Thorne, they showcased dominance throughout, including a record-breaking 96–19 win over the Waratahs. Despite a loss to the Blues in the 2003 final, the Crusaders rebounded in 2004, finishing second in the table. The 2005 season saw them reclaim the title, defeating the Waratahs 35–20 in the final.

    Super 14 Expansion and Success: 2006–2010

    In 2006, the Super 12 expanded to the Super 14, introducing new teams. The Crusaders faced their only loss but went on to win the title, defeating the Hurricanes 19–12 in a foggy final. The following years saw consistent success, with victories in 2008 and a strong run in 2009, finishing with the best defensive record despite a semi-final loss to the Bulls. The 2010 season ended with a semi-final defeat against the Bulls.

    Overcoming Challenges and Triumphs: 2011–2016

    The 2011 season was marked by the devastating Christchurch earthquake, but the Crusaders showcased resilience, reaching the final but falling short against the Reds. Despite a semi-final loss in 2012, the team consistently made the playoffs. In 2017, under coach Scott Robertson, the Crusaders secured their eighth title, defeating the Lions 25–17 in the final.

    Three Consecutive Titles: 2018–2019

    Under Robertson's leadership, the Crusaders continued their dominance, securing their ninth title in 2018 by defeating the Lions 37–18. The trend continued in 2019, as they clinched their third consecutive title, defeating the Jaguares 19–3 at home.

    Recent Glory: 2020–2023

    The COVID-19 pandemic disrupted the 2020 season, but the Crusaders returned in Super Rugby Aotearoa, winning seven out of eight games. They secured their fifth consecutive title in 2021, defeating the Chiefs 24–13 in the final. In 2022 and 2023, the Crusaders continued their success, winning their 11th and 12th Super Rugby titles in the newly formed Super Rugby Pacific competition. The 2023 Grand Final marked the end of an era, solidifying the Scott Robertson dynasty.
