    • About Zebre Parma

    Zebre Parma, founded in 2012, is an Italian professional rugby union team based in Parma, Emilia-Romagna. Competing in the United Rugby Championship, Zebre represents a collaboration between the Italian Rugby Federation and the Emilia-Romagna Rugby Committee. The team's distinctive name, "Zebre," meaning zebras in Italian, reflects their black and white striped jerseys. As one of the two Italian franchises in the United Rugby Championship, Zebre Parma plays their home matches at the Stadio Sergio Lanfranchi. Over the years, Zebre has contributed players to the Italian national team, showcasing their role in developing rugby talent within Italy.

    Zebre Parma: Italian Rugby Powerhouse

    Zebre Parma, founded in 2012, is a dynamic force in Italian professional rugby, competing fiercely in the United Rugby Championship and EPCR competitions. Representing the vibrant regions of Emilia-Romagna, Liguria, Lombardy, Piedmont, and more, Zebre earned the nickname "the XV of the North-West." This powerhouse, operated by the Italian Rugby Federation, emerged in the 2021–22 season as Zebre Parma, showcasing its commitment to excellence. Despite a challenging start in the Pro12 and Heineken Cup, Zebre's determination and improved performance in subsequent seasons highlight their growth and resilience.

    Historic Entry into Celtic League: 2010–11 Season

    Zebre's journey into top-tier competition began with Italy's entry into the Celtic League, a proposal that faced hurdles but ultimately materialized in the 2010–11 season. The Italian teams, including Zebre, brought a new dynamic to the league, securing places in the coveted Heineken Cup. This move aimed to provide Italian players with a competitive platform domestically and enhance Italy's performance in the Six Nations Championship. Despite initial challenges and debates, the inclusion of Italian teams marked a significant step forward for the nation's rugby landscape.

    Transition from Aironi to Zebre: 2012

    Zebre's inception followed the struggles of Aironi, the previous Italian franchise. Aironi faced financial difficulties, leading to the revocation of their licenses for both the European Cup and Pro12. In June 2012, the Italian Rugby Federation unveiled Zebre as the new franchise based in Parma, emphasizing the federation's control over the revitalized team. While Zebre faced initial challenges, including winless campaigns, their subsequent seasons showcased improvement, with notable victories against strong opponents like Cardiff Blues and Ospreys.

    Building Success: 2013–16 Seasons

    Zebre's resilience and commitment to growth became evident in the 2013–14 season when they secured their first Pro12 victory against Cardiff Blues. The subsequent seasons saw notable achievements, including back-to-back victories against Treviso and a crucial bonus-point win against Newport Gwent Dragons in the 2015–16 season. Zebre's ability to avoid finishing last marked a turning point, demonstrating their capability to compete at a higher level.
