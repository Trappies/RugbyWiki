    • About Bayonne

    Aviron Bayonnais, commonly known as Aviron Bayonne, is a French rugby union club based in Bayonne, in the Basque Country. Founded in 1904, the club has a rich history and a passionate fan base. Aviron Bayonne has consistently competed in the top tiers of French rugby and is known for its distinctive blue and white striped jerseys. The team plays its home matches at the iconic Stade Jean-Dauger. Over the years, the club has experienced periods of success, including notable achievements in domestic and European competitions. Aviron Bayonne holds a significant place in the rugby landscape, contributing to the vibrant rugby culture in the Basque region and maintaining a strong connection with its loyal supporters.

    Aviron Bayonnais - French Rugby Club

    Aviron Bayonnais, affectionately known as Bayonne, is a historic French rugby union club based in Bayonne, Pyrénées-Atlantiques. Established in 1904, the club has a rich legacy and strong ties to the French Basque community. Competing in the top tiers, Bayonne plays its home matches at the renowned Parc des Sports, also known as Stade Jean-Dauger. The team's distinctive blue and white striped jerseys are donned as they passionately represent their region. Despite facing relegation challenges, Bayonne's resilience and historical successes make them a notable entity in French rugby.

    Historic Triumphs and Challenges

    Aviron Bayonnais has a storied history in French rugby, making its first final appearance in 1913 and securing victory against S.C.U.F. Since then, the club has faced both triumphs and challenges. Notable successes include championship victories in the 1930s, marked by a memorable win against Biarritz and a Challenge Yves du Manoir triumph in 1936. Despite a period of lesser success, Bayonne reemerged in the 1980s, winning the Challenge Yves du Manoir in 1980 and reaching the championship final in 1982. The team's journey, marked by relegations and promotions, showcases their resilience and commitment to the sport.

    Symbolic Mascot and Community Ties

    Founded in 1904, Aviron Bayonnais not only boasts a strong rugby legacy but also holds symbolic connections to its community. The club's mascot, a pottok pony named Pottoka, adds a distinctive touch to their identity. This reflects the team's rootedness in the local culture and its commitment to embodying the spirit of the French Basque region. With ties to the community and a rich heritage, Aviron Bayonnais remains a symbol of pride for its supporters and the broader French rugby landscape.
