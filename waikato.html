    • About Waikato

    Established in 1921, the Waikato rugby team boasts a rich history in New Zealand's sporting landscape. Competing in the National Provincial Championship, Waikato has been a powerhouse, consistently showcasing exceptional skill and determination. Home games are played at FMG Stadium Waikato, a venue that resonates with the echoes of rugby legends. Aligned with the Chiefs Super Rugby franchise, Waikato takes pride in their distinctive red, yellow, and black colors. With a commitment to excellence and a tradition of producing top-tier talent, Waikato continues to be a formidable force, etching its name in the annals of New Zealand rugby history.

    Origins and Evolution

    Founded in 1921, the Waikato rugby team's roots run deep in New Zealand's rugby heritage. The modern team emerged in 1976 when they entered the inaugural National Provincial Championship, now known as the Bunnings NPC.

    Triumphs Through the Years

    With a legacy of success, Waikato has claimed the NPC title three times – in 1992, 2006, and 2021. Beyond these overall victories, the team has consistently excelled, securing numerous divisional titles over the years.

    Present Glory in the Bunnings NPC

    Competing in the premier division of the Bunnings NPC, Waikato proudly calls FMG Stadium Waikato its home. With a capacity of 25,000, the stadium becomes a fortress as Waikato continues to add to its storied rugby history.
