    • About North Harbour

    Established in 1985, the North Harbour rugby team represents the vibrant Northern Shore of Auckland, New Zealand. Competing in the National Provincial Championship (NPC), this professional rugby union team has become synonymous with skill and tenacity. The team proudly sports their colors, featuring a striking combination of green, black, and white, as they fiercely defend their turf at QBE Stadium in Albany, the team's fortress. Affiliated with the Blues Super Rugby franchise, North Harbour's legacy is etched in the annals of New Zealand rugby, embodying the spirit of excellence and competition that defines the NPC.

    North Harbour Rugby Union: Pinnacle of Northern Shore Rugby

    Established in 1985, the North Harbour Rugby Union (NHRU) governs a vast region north of Auckland, boasting 12 formidable rugby clubs. The union, known as North Harbour or simply Harbour, carved its niche by breaking away from the Auckland Rugby Union, symbolized by the Hibiscus flower in its logo, paying homage to the Hibiscus Coast. The NHRU provincial rugby team competes in the prestigious Mitre 10 Cup, showcasing prowess since its inception. Their journey includes triumphs in the 3rd division, promotions to NPC Division 1, and memorable runs in the 1990s NPC competitions. With home games played at the iconic North Harbour Stadium at Albany, North Harbour proudly contributes players to the Super Rugby competition, embodying a rich legacy of success and resilience.

    NHRU History: The Birth and Growth of a Rugby Powerhouse

    In 1985, North Harbour Rugby Union emerged, led by clubs breaking away from the Auckland Rugby Union. Their inaugural under 20 side marked the beginning, with Grant Ferguson securing the honor of scoring the union's first points. Over the years, the union expanded, absorbing clubs like Omaha, Warkworth, and Kaipara Flats, forging the Mahurangi club. North Harbour's journey includes hosting the 1994 NPC final against Auckland, Super 10 competition qualifications, and consistent contributions to New Zealand Representative players.

    North Harbour's Mitre 10 Cup Campaigns: Triumphs and Challenges

    North Harbour's presence in the Mitre 10 Cup has been marked by triumphs and challenges. The team, playing in striking green, black, and white colors, made history as the first to face the Tasman Makos, securing a historic Ranfurly Shield victory over Canterbury in 2006. However, subsequent years brought ups and downs, from disappointments in 2007 to significant wins against champions like Canterbury in 2009. The team's journey includes promotions, heartbreaking defeats, and an impressive campaign in 2017, with notable victories against Auckland and Waikato.

    Promotion to Premiership Division: North Harbour's Resurgence

    In 2016, North Harbour's exceptional performance saw them promoted from the Championship division. Victories over Counties Manukau, Manawatu, Southland, Bay of Plenty, and Northland culminated in their triumphant promotion. The journey continued in 2017 as they secured wins against formidable opponents, making the semi-finals but falling to Canterbury. The team's resilience and skill were further showcased in the 2018 season, with victories over Northland, Waikato, Bay of Plenty, Hawke's Bay, Taranaki, and Counties Manukau. North Harbour's journey in New Zealand rugby reflects a legacy of determination, growth, and a commitment to excellence.
