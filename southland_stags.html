    • About Southland Stags

    The Southland Rugby Football Union, proudly represented by the Southland Stags, stands as a testament to unwavering determination in New Zealand's rugby landscape. Established in 1887, the union has been a resilient force in the National Provincial Championship (NPC) since its inception in 1976. The Stags, adorned in maroon and gold, embody the spirit of Southland as they fiercely compete in the NPC, showcasing a legacy of grit and passion for the game. With home games hosted at Rugby Park Stadium in Invercargill, the Southland Stags enjoy fervent support from their local community, creating an atmosphere that echoes the rich rugby heritage of the region. Affiliated with the Highlanders Super Rugby franchise, the Southland Stags serve as a crucial pathway for emerging talent, contributing to the broader success of New Zealand rugby. The maroon and gold colors proudly worn by the Stags symbolize not just a team but a community's enduring commitment to the sport they hold dear.

    Southland Rugby Union: Founded in 1887

    Established in 1887, the Southland Rugby Union has a rich history in New Zealand's rugby landscape. Originally founded in 1887, the union has been a key participant in the National Provincial Championship since its inception in 1976.

    Home Ground: Rugby Park Stadium, Invercargill

    The Southland Stags call Rugby Park Stadium in Invercargill their home. This venue hosts the team's home games, providing a vibrant and supportive environment for players and fans alike.

    Affiliation with Highlanders in Super Rugby

    As a crucial component of player development, the Southland Stags are affiliated with the Highlanders Super Rugby franchise. This collaboration serves as a talent pipeline, contributing to the broader success of New Zealand rugby.

    Colors and Identity: Maroon and Gold

    Identified by their distinctive maroon and gold colors, the Southland Stags proudly showcase their unique identity on the rugby field. These colors symbolize the unity and strength of the Southland rugby community.
