    • About Seattle Seawolves

    The Seattle Seawolves, established in 2017, have become a dominant force in American rugby, competing fiercely in Major League Rugby (MLR) and capturing the championship in the league's inaugural 2018 season. Playing their home games at Starfire Stadium in Tukwila, Washington, the Seawolves boast a dedicated fan base and actively engage with the community through youth development programs and outreach initiatives. Beyond their on-field success, the Seawolves stand as a symbol of excellence and community spirit, contributing significantly to the growth of rugby in the United States.

    Seattle Seawolves Legacy

    The Seattle Seawolves, founded in 2017 by an investor group led by Adrian Balfour and Shane Skinner, quickly rose to prominence in Major League Rugby (MLR). They clinched back-to-back championships in the league's first two seasons, securing the inaugural MLR Grand Final in 2018 and successfully defending their title in the 2019 Championship against San Diego Legion. This early success solidified the Seawolves as a dominant force in American rugby.

    Starfire Sports Fortress

    Nestled in Tukwila, Washington, the Seattle Seawolves call Starfire Sports Complex their home. The 4,500-seat stadium, primarily catering to soccer teams, provides an electric atmosphere for rugby enthusiasts. In their inaugural season, the Seawolves sold out 1,800 season tickets, showcasing the passionate support from their dedicated fan base.

    Broadcast Excellence

    For the 2019 season, the Seawolves' home games were broadcast on Root Sports Northwest, an AT&T SportsNet affiliate. The dynamic commentary duo of Dan Power and Kevin Swiryn brought the thrilling on-field action to fans, contributing to the team's growing popularity beyond the confines of Starfire Sports.
