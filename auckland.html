    • About Auckland

    The Auckland rugby team stands as a cornerstone in the vibrant tapestry of New Zealand rugby, with a legacy stretching back to the establishment of the Auckland Rugby Football Union in 1883. As a powerhouse in the National Provincial Championship, Auckland has consistently showcased a brand of rugby marked by skill, tenacity, and a relentless pursuit of victory. Their home games unfold at the iconic Eden Park, a fortress echoing with the cheers of passionate supporters. The team's affiliation with the Blues Super Rugby franchise further solidifies its role as a nurturing ground for top-tier talent. Dressed in their distinctive blue and white jerseys, Auckland rugby players carry not just the weight of history but also the aspirations of a community deeply connected to the sport.

    Auckland Rugby Legacy at Eden Park

    Established in 1883, Auckland Rugby, clad in iconic blue and white hoops, commands the historic Eden Park in Auckland, New Zealand. This rugby powerhouse, affiliated with the Blues Super Rugby franchise, boasts a storied history. From the early days of the union to the reformed National Provincial Championship, Auckland's legacy is etched in the annals of New Zealand rugby.

    Blues Affiliation: Auckland's Super Rugby Connection

    In the realm of Super Rugby, Auckland proudly aligns itself with the Blues franchise. A synergy of talent and tradition, this affiliation enhances the rugby landscape. The blue and white hoops of Auckland seamlessly blend with the Super Rugby flair, showcasing a harmonious connection between grassroots excellence and professional prowess.
