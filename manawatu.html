    • About Manawatu

    Established in 1886, the Manawatu Rugby Football Union has been a pillar of New Zealand's rugby legacy for over a century. The team, often referred to as the Manawatu Turbos, competes in the National Provincial Championship (NPC), showcasing a proud history of resilience and skill on the rugby field. With a home base at Central Energy Trust Arena in Palmerston North, the Turbos draw passionate support from the Manawatu community. Their participation in the NPC reflects a commitment to excellence, and they continue to contribute significantly to the development of rugby talent in the region. As a key player in New Zealand's rugby landscape, the Manawatu Turbos stand as a testament to the enduring spirit of the sport.

    Manawatu Turbos: A Rugby Legacy Since 1886

    Established in 1886, the Manawatu Rugby Football Union has a rich history deeply embedded in New Zealand's rugby heritage. Known as the Manawatu Turbos, this professional rugby union team competes with pride in the National Provincial Championship (NPC), showcasing a commitment to excellence that spans over a century. The team's vibrant green and white colors symbolize their connection to the Manawatū-Whanganui region, and their home games at Central Energy Trust Arena in Palmerston North draw fervent support from the local community.

    National Provincial Championship Glory

    The Manawatu Turbos, a powerhouse in New Zealand rugby, have been a consistent force since the establishment of the National Provincial Championship in 1976. With a legacy dating back to 1886, the Turbos have embraced the challenges and triumphs of the NPC, contributing significantly to the tournament's storied history. Their green and white jersey represents not just a team but a region's passion for the sport.

    Palmerston North's Pride: Central Energy Trust Arena

    Central Energy Trust Arena in Palmerston North serves as the hallowed ground where the Manawatu Turbos showcase their prowess. As the team's home base, this stadium resonates with the cheers and roars of passionate fans, creating an electric atmosphere during Turbos' home games. The arena stands as a testament to the deep connection between the team and the local community, fostering a sense of pride and unity.

    Affiliated with Hurricanes: A Pathway to Super Rugby

    The Manawatu Turbos serve as a crucial pathway to Super Rugby, affiliated with the Hurricanes franchise. This collaboration underscores the Turbos' role in nurturing and developing top-tier rugby talent, providing a stepping stone for players to reach greater heights in the professional rugby landscape. The green and white colors seamlessly connect the Turbos to the Hurricanes' vibrant rugby legacy.

    Manawatu Turbos: Green and White Warriors

    Draped in the iconic green and white, the Manawatu Turbos embody the warrior spirit on the rugby field. With roots dating back to 1886, the Turbos continue to be a formidable presence in New Zealand's rugby scene. Their participation in the reformed NPC is a testament to their enduring legacy and unwavering commitment to the game. The Manawatu Turbos stand tall, a symbol of rugby excellence in the Manawatū-Whanganui region.
