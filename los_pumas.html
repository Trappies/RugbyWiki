    • About Los Pumas

    The Argentina national rugby team, often referred to as "Los Pumas", is a formidable force in international rugby. Established in 1910, the team has steadily grown in prominence and skill, becoming a key contender on the global stage. Known for their passionate and resilient style of play, the Pumas have achieved notable successes, including reaching the semi-finals of the Rugby World Cup in 2007 and consistently competing in prestigious tournaments like The Rugby Championship. Argentina's rugby prowess is deeply rooted in a rich history of the sport, with a growing pool of talented players who have made a significant impact on the world of rugby. With a fervent fan base and a commitment to excellence, the Pumas continue to be a team to watch, embodying the spirit and tenacity of Argentine rugby.

    Origins and Nickname

    The history of the Argentina national rugby union team dates back to 1910 when they played their first international match against the British Isles. The tour marked the beginning of Argentina's recognition in rugby, and the team earned the nickname "Los Pumas," which remains an iconic identity to this day.

    Early Years and First International Triumph

    In the early 20th century, rugby took root in Argentina, with Irish immigrants playing a pivotal role. The first rugby union match in 1873 laid the foundation for the sport's growth. Argentina's journey included defeats by the British Lions in 1927, but the team wore its traditional light blue and white jersey for the first time, leaving a lasting mark.

    The Birth of Los Pumas

    The 1960s marked a turning point for Argentina's rugby team with tours by the home unions. The team's first trip across the Atlantic in 1965 introduced the nickname "Pumas" after a journalist's mix-up. Victories over the Junior Springboks and the emergence of key players set the stage for a new era in Argentine rugby.

    Steady Rise and International Tours (1970s)

    Led by fly-half Hugo Porta, Argentina saw a steady rise in the 1970s. International tours to South Africa, Ireland, and Scotland showcased their prowess. The tour of England, Ireland, and Italy in 1979 demonstrated Argentina's growing importance on the global rugby stage.

    Golden Year of 1985 and International Tours (1980s)

    1985 stood out as a landmark year for Los Pumas, achieving a historic tie against the All Blacks. Tours by England and France in the '80s highlighted Argentina's growth. The team's performance against Australia in 1987 showcased their competitiveness, laying the foundation for future successes.

    World Cup Achievements and Transition (1990s)

    The '90s witnessed a transition phase for Argentina, with the retirement of experienced players. Despite challenges, the team's powerful pack earned praise in the 1995 World Cup. The 1999 World Cup marked a significant milestone as Los Pumas reached the quarter-finals for the first time.

    New Millennium Challenges and World Cup Campaigns (2000s)

    In the early 2000s, coach Marcelo Loffreda led Argentina through challenges, including a narrow loss to Ireland in the 2003 World Cup. The team's form against Wales and France showcased their resilience. The 2007 Rugby World Cup saw Los Pumas impress with a quarter-final finish, led by standout performances from Gonzalo Quesada.

    Continued Success and International Tests (2000s-2010s)

    The 2000s and 2010s saw Argentina's continued success, marked by victories over Wales, England, and Ireland. Notable performances against the British & Irish Lions and All Blacks cemented Argentina's status as a formidable rugby nation. The team's qualification for the expanded Tri Nations hinted at even greater challenges and achievements in the years to come.

    Argentina's Remarkable Journey in the 2007 Rugby World Cup

    In the 2007 Rugby World Cup, Argentina, or Los Pumas, achieved historic feats that reverberated globally. Defeating hosts France in the opening match set the tone for an impressive run, culminating in a 34–10 win against France in the 3rd/4th place playoff—Argentina's best-ever Rugby World Cup performance. The Pumas' success prompted discussions about increasing Argentina's role in international rugby and led to unprecedented scheduling adjustments in football-crazed Argentina.

    2011 World Cup: Pumas' Resilience and Quarterfinal Qualification

    In the 2011 Rugby World Cup, Los Pumas showcased resilience, securing a quarterfinal spot with victories against Romania and Scotland. Despite a 33–10 defeat to the All Blacks in the quarterfinals, Argentina's campaign was deemed a success, marking their first consecutive quarterfinal qualification in World Cup history.

    Argentina Joins The Rugby Championship in 2012

    A significant milestone occurred in 2012 as Argentina joined The Rugby Championship, facing rugby powerhouses New Zealand, Australia, and South Africa. Despite challenges, including a historic 16–16 draw against South Africa, Argentina earned respect for their competitive spirit, marking their debut in top-tier international competition.

    Ups and Downs in the Daniel Hourcade Era

    Under coach Daniel Hourcade from 2013, Los Pumas experienced triumphs like their first-ever Rugby Championship win against Australia in 2014. However, they faced challenges, finishing last in the tournament for three consecutive years. The journey included ups, like defeating South Africa in the 2015 Rugby Championship, and downs, including a record 73–13 defeat to South Africa in 2013.

    2015 World Cup: Pumas' Entertaining Rugby and Semi-final Glory

    In the 2015 Rugby World Cup, Argentina showcased an entertaining and attacking style, scoring 22 tries. While falling short with a 29–15 defeat to Australia in the semi-finals, Los Pumas' journey marked another successful campaign, reaching the semi-finals for the second time in Rugby World Cup history. Argentina's rugby story epitomizes passion, resilience, and a commitment to global competitiveness.
