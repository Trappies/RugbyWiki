    • About The Glasgow Warriors

    Glasgow Warriors, founded in 1996, are a professional rugby union team based in Glasgow, Scotland. Competing in the United Rugby Championship and the European Rugby Champions Cup, the team plays their home games at Scotstoun Stadium. Since their inception, the Warriors have become a formidable force in Scottish rugby, consistently contending in domestic and international competitions. They clinched their first major trophy in the 2014-15 season by winning the Pro12 (now United Rugby Championship). Known for their passionate fanbase and dynamic style of play, the Glasgow Warriors continue to make significant contributions to the Scottish rugby scene.

    Origins and Professionalization (1996-2005)

    Glasgow Warriors trace their roots to the 1872 Glasgow District side, transforming into a professional entity in 1996 as Glasgow Rugby. The club underwent various rebrands, becoming Glasgow Caledonians in 1998 through a merger with Caledonian Reds, then reverting to Glasgow Rugby in 2001 and adopting the name Glasgow Warriors in 2005.

    Mergers and Challenges (1998-2005)

    Financial constraints led to a merger with Caledonia Reds in 1998, briefly named Glasgow Caledonians. The 2001–02 season saw a return to Glasgow Rugby, while the moniker "Glasgow Warriors" gained prominence. The period featured mixed success, including participation in European competitions.

    Celtic League and 1872 Cup (2005-2011)

    Rebranded as Glasgow Warriors in 2005, the team entered the Celtic League. The 1872 Cup, introduced in 2007-08 against Edinburgh Rugby, marked a historic inter-city rivalry. During the Pro12 era, the Warriors consistently reached the play-offs, securing a record number of consecutive seasons.

    Pro14 Era and Records (2011-2021)

    The Celtic League became the Pro12 in 2011–12. Glasgow Warriors consistently excelled, securing play-off spots, with a notable runner-up finish in 2018. The 2019–20 season saw them clinch the 1872 Cup and achieve multiple records despite the pandemic disruptions.

    United Rugby Championship and Franco Smith Era (2021-2022)

    In the expanded Pro14, renamed United Rugby Championship (URC), Glasgow Warriors faced challenges. A slump in form led to an 8th-place finish, missing Champions Cup qualification. Head Coach Danny Wilson was replaced by Franco Smith ahead of the 2022-23 season.

    Franco Smith's Impact (2022-)

    Under Franco Smith's leadership, Glasgow Warriors experienced a revival in the 2022-23 season. Unbeaten at home in the URC, they reached the European Challenge Cup final, securing the 1872 Cup and the Scottish-Italian Shield. Despite setbacks, Smith's tenure marked a successful resurgence, with the team back in contention across competitions.
