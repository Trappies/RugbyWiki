    • About The Scarlets

    The Scarlets, based in Llanelli, Wales, are a professional rugby union team with a rich history and a reputation for exciting and expansive play. Formed in 2003 following the regionalization of Welsh rugby, the Scarlets represent the counties of Carmarthenshire, Pembrokeshire, and Ceredigion. With a distinctive red jersey, they play their home matches at Parc y Scarlets. Over the years, the Scarlets have been a powerhouse in Welsh rugby, achieving success in domestic and European competitions. Renowned for their commitment to attacking rugby, the Scarlets have produced numerous international stars and played a pivotal role in shaping the modern era of Welsh regional rugby.

    Formation of the Scarlets

    In 2003, the Welsh Rugby Union (WRU) underwent a restructuring, reducing the top tier of Welsh professional rugby from nine clubs to five regions. The Scarlets, officially launched on July 7, 2003, initially represented the whole of West and North Wales. However, their presence in North Wales diminished over the years, and as of 2018, the Scarlets consider their region to represent the three counties of Carmarthenshire, Ceredigion, and Pembrokeshire.

    Early Years (2003–2014)

    The Scarlets made an impressive start in the 2003–04 season, winning the Celtic League and reaching the quarter-finals of the Heineken Cup. However, the following seasons saw challenges, including key player departures and injuries, affecting their league and Heineken Cup performances. Despite these struggles, they found success in reaching the final of the Celtic Cup in 2005.

    Move to Parc y Scarlets (2008–09 Season)

    The Scarlets bid farewell to Stradey Park in 2008, moving to the new Parc y Scarlets in Pemberton. This marked a significant transition, and their inaugural match at the new stadium was held against Ulster. The official opening ceremony took place on January 31, 2009.

    Pivotal Moments (2016–17 Season)

    The 2016–17 season brought notable achievements, with the Scarlets finishing third in the league and securing a place in the play-offs. They went on to defeat Leinster and Munster, clinching the league title with a convincing 46–22 win in the final. This success marked their second league title, the first being in 2004.

    Coaching Changes and Recent Developments (2019 Onwards)

    The coaching landscape saw changes, with Brad Mooar starting as head coach in 2019 but leaving mid-season. Dwayne Peel took over, and in the 2021–22 season, Hugh Hogan joined as defence coach. The 2022–23 season showcased a mix of challenges and triumphs, including victories in the EPCR Challenge Cup and notable signings like Vaea Fifita. However, the season concluded with a semi-final loss.

    2023–24 Season and Restructuring

    The upcoming season sees promising additions to the Scarlets, including Welsh international Ioan Lloyd and returning player Tomi Lewis. The coaching staff welcomes Jared Payne as the new attack coach and Albert van den Berg as the forwards coach. Despite challenges such as reduced salary caps imposed by the WRU, the Scarlets remain committed to developing talent from their academy, emphasizing a blend of experience and emerging talent for the future.
