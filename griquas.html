    • About The Griquas

    Griquas, an enduring force in South African rugby, holds a legacy defined by resilience and commitment. Originating in Kimberley, the Griquas Rugby Union was formed in 1886, making it one of the oldest rugby unions in the country. The team proudly wears the iconic orange jersey, symbolizing their distinctive identity. Griquas have navigated the challenges of South African rugby history, contributing significantly to the sport's fabric. Their commitment to excellence is showcased in their participation in premier competitions like the Currie Cup and Rugby Challenge. The team's home ground, Tafel Lager Park, resonates with the echoes of passionate supporters. Griquas, with their rich heritage, continue to play a vital role in shaping the narrative of South African rugby.

    Griquas Rugby: Crafting a Currie Cup Legacy

    Griquas, adorned in the vibrant hues of the Windhoek Draught sponsorship since April 2022, stand as a stalwart in South African rugby. Established in 1886 in Griqualand West, their journey is etched with historical significance. In 1891, during the British Lions tour, Griqualand West's commendable performance earned them the prestigious Currie Cup. The team clinched the Currie Cup thrice, in 1899, 1911, and 1970, solidifying their prowess. Griqua Park in Kimberley resonates with the cheers of their ardent supporters, mostly from the Northern Cape Province. The 'Peacock Blues' engage in enduring rivalries with Western Province and the Free State Cheetahs, adding layers to their storied narrative.

    Griquas' Currie Cup Triumphs: 1899, 1911, 1970

    Griquas, a force in South African rugby, boasts an illustrious history marked by three triumphant Currie Cup campaigns. The inaugural victory came in 1899 when Griqualand West's rugby prowess earned them the coveted Currie Cup during the British Lions tour. In 1911, Griquas once again claimed the championship, etching their name in the annals of rugby history. The crowning moment arrived in 1970, as Griquas, with resilience and skill, secured their third Currie Cup title. These victories not only showcase Griquas' on-field excellence but also contribute to the rich tapestry of South African rugby heritage.

    Griquas vs. Western Province: A Timeless Rivalry

    The rivalry between Griquas and Western Province transcends the sands of time, dating back to the early days of the Currie Cup. Griqualand West's dominance in South African rugby forged this fierce competition, adding a layer of intensity to every encounter. The battles on the rugby field between Griquas and Western Province are etched with the passion of supporters, creating moments that reverberate through the rugby corridors of history.

    The 'Central Derby': Griquas vs. Free State Cheetahs

    In the heart of South African rugby, a friendly rivalry blooms between Griquas and the Free State Cheetahs, dubbed the 'central derby.' Since the 1970s, these two teams have engaged in spirited clashes, each vying for supremacy in the rugby landscape. The central derby adds a dynamic chapter to Griquas' competitive narrative, with matches filled with intensity, skill, and the shared love for the sport.

    Griquas: The 'Peacock Blues'

    Griquas, affectionately known as the 'Peacock Blues,' stand as a symbol of pride and resilience. The team's distinctive blue jerseys, sponsored by Windhoek Draught since April 2022, symbolize their unique identity. Griquas' passionate supporters, predominantly from the Northern Cape Province, adorn themselves in the 'Peacock Blues,' celebrating the team's legacy and contributing to the vibrant atmosphere at Griqua Park in Kimberley.
