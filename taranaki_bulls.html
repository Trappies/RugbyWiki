    • About Taranaki Bulls

    The Taranaki Bulls, representing the Taranaki Rugby Football Union in New Zealand, are a formidable presence in domestic rugby. Competing in the Mitre 10 Cup, the Bulls showcase their skill and tenacity on the field. Taranaki has a rich rugby history, and the Bulls have been successful in securing titles in various competitions. Known for their distinctive amber and black jerseys, the Bulls contribute talented players to Super Rugby franchises, particularly the Chiefs. With a strong fanbase and a tradition of producing top-tier rugby talent, the Taranaki Bulls continue to make their mark in New Zealand rugby.

    Taranaki Rugby Union

    Established in 1889, Taranaki Rugby Union is the governing body for rugby in the Taranaki region of New Zealand. Representing the Mitre 10 Cup side, Taranaki Bulls, and the Farah Palmer Cup side, Taranaki Whio, the union has a rich history and is affiliated with the Chiefs Super Rugby franchise. Their iconic amber and black jerseys are donned during home games at the TET Stadium & Events Centre in Inglewood.

    Early Years

    Taranaki's rugby legacy began officially in 1889, adopting the amber and black hoops in 1892. During the amateur era, they clinched the Ranfurly Shield four times and secured seven second division titles in the National Provincial Championship. Memorable moments include the record-breaking 1959 match against the British Lions, drawing 35,000 spectators, and the 1963 Ranfurly Shield victory over Wellington.

    Ranfurly Shield Success

    The first Ranfurly Shield triumph occurred in 1913, followed by successful tenures in 1957, 1963, and 1996. In 2011, Taranaki embarked on its fifth shield tenure, defeating Southland 15-12, but the reign concluded in 2012 with a loss to Waikato. Notably, Taranaki RFU formed a women's professional team in 2013, clinching their maiden national title in 2014.
