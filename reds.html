    • About The Reds

    The Queensland Reds, based in Brisbane, Australia, are a professional rugby union team with a rich history and a formidable presence in the sport. Established in 1882, the Reds have been a powerhouse in Australian rugby, contributing significantly to the country's rugby legacy. The team competes in various domestic and international competitions, including Super Rugby, where they have achieved notable success. With a vibrant maroon jersey, the Reds boast a loyal fan base and a reputation for dynamic, skillful play on the field. Over the years, the Reds have produced legendary players and played a crucial role in the development of rugby in Australia. As one of the nation's premier rugby teams, the Queensland Reds continue to uphold a tradition of excellence and competitiveness, making them a celebrated force in the world of rugby union.

    Early Queensland Years: Building the Rugby Foundation

    In the late 19th century, rugby in Queensland had a tumultuous journey. The Brisbane Football Club transitioned to rugby in 1876, aligning with new clubs like 'Rangers' and 'Bonnet Rouge.' The game evolved with local variations, sparking a code battle. In 1883, the Queensland [Rugby] side's victory against NSW marked a turning point, solidifying rugby's popularity. The team's success prompted the formation of the Northern Rugby Union in 1883. Subsequent years witnessed increased popularity, with inter-colonial matches and a historic win against The Lions in 1899.

    Pre-Super Competitions: Resilience Amid Challenges

    World War I and the emergence of rugby league led to a dormant period for Queensland rugby. The Queensland Rugby Union (QRU) disbanded in 1919, reviving in the late 1920s. Ballymore Stadium became the spiritual home in 1966. In 1980, a milestone was achieved as Queensland defeated the All Blacks for the first time. The 1990s marked a transition, with Queensland Reds participating in Super Rugby's early years.

    Early Super Rugby: Back-to-Back Champions

    In the inaugural Super 10 in 1993, Queensland Reds finished fourth. The following years were marked by success, with back-to-back championships in 1994 and 1995. Super 12's inception in 1996 saw Queensland top the table. The 1996 semi-final against the Sharks at Ballymore was a notable moment. However, the 1997 season saw them finish ninth. The late 1990s and early 2000s showcased mixed results, with notable performances in 1999 and 2001.

    Super 14 Era: Ups and Downs

    The transition to Super 14 in 2006 saw Queensland finish 12th. Under coach Eddie Jones, there was a resurgence in 2007, marked by defeating the Brumbies but ending with a wooden spoon. The 2008 season showed promise, with notable victories. The 2010 Super 14 highlighted Queensland's potential, missing the finals but setting the stage for future success.

    Super Rugby: The Renaissance

    The debut season of the revamped Super Rugby in 2011 witnessed Queensland Reds' remarkable transformation. Under coach Ewen McKenzie, they finished at the top of the table and secured their first Super Rugby Championship, defeating the Crusaders in a historic final at Suncorp Stadium. Despite subsequent challenges, including lower finishes in the following years, the Reds continued to evolve. The appointment of Brad Thorn as head coach in 2018 marked a commitment to rejuvenating the franchise. The Reds' success in Super Rugby AU in 2020 and their impressive performance in 2021 showcased their resilience and determination.
